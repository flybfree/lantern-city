# Lantern City — MVP Reference Case

## Purpose

This document defines the first fully authored reference case for Lantern City.
It is meant to function as:
- the gold-standard example case for the MVP
- a test of the player interaction model
- a reference for NPC design, clue structure, lantern effects, and fallout
- a baseline for future procedural or semi-procedural case generation

This case should be playable inside the MVP without requiring the full city simulation.
It is intentionally scoped to prove the core loop works.

## Case Summary

### Title
The Missing Clerk

### Short pitch
A records clerk in the Old Quarter has gone missing after a lantern outage near the archive. The obvious explanation is disappearance, abduction, or flight. The true problem is subtler: the clerk was not only taken out of sight, but partially edited out of the civic memory structure around the archive district.

### What this case is meant to prove
The case should demonstrate that Lantern City can support:
- one coherent city seed
- one central case with multiple scenes
- NPCs with motives and memory
- clues with changing reliability
- lantern state affecting what can be known
- access as progression
- partial success, failure, and political resolution
- persistent fallout after case closure

## Case Classification

- Case size: medium MVP case
- Primary district: Old Quarter
- Secondary district: Lantern Ward or a nearby administrative edge location
- Primary case type: missing person plus civic inconsistency
- Core systems exercised:
  - conversation
  - clue discovery
  - clue contradiction
  - lantern inspection
  - access gating
  - reputation and leverage
  - case pressure
  - fallout

## Core Mystery

### Surface mystery
A low-ranking archive clerk named Tovin Vale has vanished after a local lantern outage. Some records still mention him. Others no longer do. Witnesses disagree on when he was last seen and whether he was even assigned to the disputed registry table.

### True underlying cause
Tovin discovered that a localized lantern had been deliberately altered to distort record continuity around a family line and a maintenance ledger. He tried to preserve evidence by copying registry discrepancies. Before he could expose the tampering, a faction-aligned intermediary used the altered lantern field and bureaucratic control to push him into a state of civic absence.

He is not fully erased from reality.
He is partially displaced:
- physically hidden in a maintenance chamber beneath an archival side building
- bureaucratically denied by altered records
- socially obscured because witnesses near the affected lantern disagree about him

### Design goal of the truth
The truth should connect:
- lantern manipulation
- civic record control
- Missingness as an institutional process
- a solvable human-scale investigation

## Thematic Function

This case introduces the player to one of Lantern City’s key truths:
The city does not only lose people in alleys or at sea.
It can lose them inside its own systems.

The case should teach the player that:
- lanterns affect more than visibility
- official records are not neutral truth
- factions can shape what counts as a real person or event
- solving a case may require deciding which truth survives publicly

## Involved Districts

## 1. Old Quarter
This is the primary investigation space.
It should contain:
- the archive steps
- shrine lane
- a side records office or ledger room
- at least one hidden or restricted maintenance route

Function in case:
- memory-heavy space
- contradictory records
- uneasy witnesses
- dim or unstable lantern influence

## 2. Lantern Ward
This is the canonical secondary support district for the MVP.
It should contain:
- one official contact point
- one records authority or lamp regulation desk
- one way to gain or deny formal legitimacy

Function in case:
- shows the official version of events
- provides pressure from authority
- lets the player pursue procedural or political solutions

## Faction Involvement

## Primary factions

### Memory Keepers
Public role:
- preserve continuity and archive integrity

Actual role in case:
- trying to contain a records anomaly before it causes wider distrust
- not uniformly guilty; different members want different outcomes

### Shrine Circles
Public role:
- maintain ritual and local lantern stability

Actual role in case:
- one local shrine keeper knows the outage was not ordinary
- shrine maintenance logs contain indirect evidence

## Secondary pressure faction

### Council of Lights or equivalent official lantern authority
Public role:
- regulate official lantern systems

Actual role in case:
- wants the disturbance resolved quietly
- may care more about public confidence than truth

## Key NPC Cast

This set is intentionally small and suitable for the MVP.

## 1. Tovin Vale
- Role category: absent lead / hidden victim
- Public identity: junior archive clerk
- Hidden truth: discovered a lantern-linked records edit and tried to preserve proof
- Current state: hidden, frightened, physically exhausted, unsure whom to trust
- Function: the person the player is trying to locate and the human anchor of the case

## 2. Ila Venn
- Role category: informant leaning lead
- Public identity: shrine keeper on Shrine Lane
- Visible goal: keep the local lantern from collapsing entirely
- Hidden goal: avoid exposing how much she already knows about the alteration
- Fear: being blamed for ritual negligence or political interference
- Function: first meaningful guide into the lantern side of the mystery

## 3. Sered Marr
- Role category: gatekeeper
- Public identity: acting archive registrar
- Visible goal: maintain order and prevent public embarrassment
- Hidden goal: contain the scandal before superiors intervene
- Fear: losing position if the altered records are traced through his office
- Function: access gate for records, source of obstruction, possible political resolution target

## 4. Brin Hesse
- Role category: world NPC who can become relevant
- Public identity: lamplighter’s assistant / maintenance runner
- Visible goal: avoid being tied to unauthorized repairs
- Hidden goal: cover for a paid errand that moved a maintenance key at the wrong time
- Fear: punishment by both the council and local crews
- Function: practical route to maintenance evidence and hidden access

## 5. Sister Calis
- Role category: secondary lead or faction voice
- Public identity: senior shrine ritualist or liaison
- Visible goal: preserve public calm
- Hidden goal: prevent the player from uncovering the full ritual signature used in the alteration
- Fear: wider scrutiny of shrine methods
- Function: escalates the political dimension and offers a partial-truth resolution path

## 6. Watcher Pell
- Role category: authority contact
- Public identity: district civic watch officer
- Visible goal: close the missing-person complaint cleanly
- Hidden goal: avoid paperwork tied to contradictory records
- Fear: being caught certifying a false official account
- Function: shows how institutional pressure turns ambiguity into denial

## Initial Player Framing

The player should receive the case through one of these equivalent openings:
- a direct request to locate a missing clerk
- a report of an archive inconsistency tied to a vanished worker
- a rumor that a man still has a desk but no name in the ledger

### Recommended MVP opening
The player is told:
- a clerk named Tovin Vale failed to report after a lantern disturbance
- the archive office insists records are unclear
- a local shrine keeper claims the outage began before his disappearance

This gives an immediate triangle:
- person
- records
- lanterns

## Opening Scene

### Location
Archive Steps, Old Quarter

### What the player sees
- damp stone steps
- a public-facing archive entrance under weak light
- a missing-person notice pinned over an older maintenance posting
- Sered Marr managing access tersely
- contradictory local gossip about whether Tovin was ever on shift there

### Immediate available actions
- Speak to Sered Marr
- Inspect the notice board and postings
- Ask nearby workers about Tovin Vale
- Travel to Shrine Lane
- Review current case

### Opening questions introduced to the player
- Did Tovin really disappear, or is something stranger happening?
- Why do the records already disagree?
- Did the outage cause the inconsistency, or cover for it?

## Core Open Questions

The case board should eventually track versions of these:
- Where is Tovin Vale now?
- Why do some records no longer show him?
- Was the lantern near the archive damaged, neglected, or altered?
- Who benefited from the record discrepancy?
- Who knew before the disappearance became public?
- Is this a local cover-up or part of a larger pattern?

## Case Pressure Model

This case should have visible pressure even in the MVP.

### Pressure sources
- archive office wants the issue contained
- local shrine fears blame
- witnesses grow less reliable the longer the lantern remains unstable
- Tovin’s condition worsens if hidden too long

### Simple pressure states
- low
- rising
- urgent

### What increases pressure
- repeated delays without progress
- public confrontation before enough evidence is gathered
- visiting authority before stabilizing local facts
- waiting after learning Tovin may still be alive

### What reduces pressure temporarily
- stabilizing the lantern site
- gaining access to better records
- securing cooperation from an informed NPC
- locating Tovin

## Scene Sequence Skeleton

This case should support several routes, but one likely sequence is:

1. Opening at Archive Steps
2. First conversation with Ila Venn at Shrine Lane
3. Investigation of lantern bracket or maintenance signs
4. Obstructed records access through Sered Marr
5. Side lead through Brin Hesse or maintenance evidence
6. Discovery of hidden access route or proof of alteration
7. Locating Tovin Vale
8. Deciding how to resolve the truth publicly
9. Fallout scene showing district reaction

This is not a strict line.
The player should be able to reorder several middle scenes.

## Clue Structure

The clue web should have both hard clues and interpretive clues.

## Core clue list

### Clue A — Contradictory roster entry
Source:
- archive postings

Meaning:
- Tovin appears on one copy and is absent on another

Initial reliability:
- credible

Likely unlocks:
- suspicion toward records tampering
- reason to inspect deeper ledgers

### Clue B — Outage predates disappearance
Source:
- Ila Venn or maintenance timing evidence

Meaning:
- the lantern problem started before Tovin officially vanished

Initial reliability:
- uncertain, then solid if corroborated

Likely unlocks:
- reframes the case from disappearance after outage to disappearance during an active manipulation

### Clue C — Heat scoring on lantern bracket
Source:
- investigation scene at Shrine Lane

Meaning:
- the lantern was altered intentionally, not simply neglected

Initial reliability:
- credible

Likely unlocks:
- Lantern Understanding gain
- ability to challenge official negligence story

### Clue D — Missing maintenance line
Source:
- ledger room or Brin Hesse testimony

Meaning:
- a maintenance visit was removed or rewritten

Initial reliability:
- uncertain

Likely unlocks:
- leverage over archive office or maintenance staff

### Clue E — Family record discrepancy
Source:
- deeper registry comparison

Meaning:
- the same lantern field may have been used to distort another record set, proving a broader motive

Initial reliability:
- contradicted until enough evidence is gathered

Likely unlocks:
- broader conspiracy interpretation
- stronger political stakes

### Clue F — Hidden copy sheet left by Tovin
Source:
- maintenance passage or hidden chamber

Meaning:
- Tovin knew the edit was deliberate and tried to preserve proof

Initial reliability:
- solid

Likely unlocks:
- direct breakthrough
- route to locating Tovin

### Clue G — Witness instability near affected light
Source:
- comparing accounts at archive steps, shrine lane, and outside the affected radius

Meaning:
- testimony conflict is spatially linked, not just social rumor

Initial reliability:
- interpretive

Likely unlocks:
- understanding of lantern reach
- stronger case for ritual alteration

## Clue Dependency Logic

The player does not need every clue to finish the case.
But some clues strongly improve confidence and resolution quality.

### Minimum breakthrough set
A player should be able to reach the truth with some combination of:
- Clue B or C
- Clue D or G
- Clue F

### Strong clean-resolution set
For the best resolution quality, the player should ideally gather:
- C
- D
- F
- at least one corroborating contradiction between A and E or A and G

## NPC Knowledge Distribution

The case should distribute truth unevenly.
No one person should explain everything.

### Ila Venn knows
- the outage was not ordinary
- the lantern signature feels altered
- someone official is lying by omission
- Tovin came asking frightened questions before vanishing

### Sered Marr knows
- the office is suppressing or rewriting record access
- there was pressure to keep the matter quiet
- some ledger inconsistencies cannot be explained publicly
- he may not know exactly where Tovin is

### Brin Hesse knows
- someone used a maintenance key off schedule
- a repair route was opened and then denied
- he carried or passed something he should not have

### Sister Calis knows
- the ritual pattern was not accidental
- exposing the full mechanism could destabilize more than one official story
- a quiet settlement is possible if the player can be contained

### Tovin knows
- he found proof of deliberate alteration
- he tried to copy enough evidence to survive denial
- he heard a specific instruction or saw a specific seal, phrase, or maintenance marker linking the event to institutional control

## Example Playable Paths

## Path 1 — Lantern-first investigation
1. Player visits Shrine Lane early.
2. Learns the outage began before the disappearance.
3. Finds deliberate tampering signs.
4. Uses lantern evidence to pressure archive access.
5. Finds hidden copy sheet.
6. Locates Tovin.
7. Chooses public or private resolution.

Primary feel:
- grows understanding through physical and ritual evidence

## Path 2 — Records-first investigation
1. Player focuses on archive contradictions.
2. Runs into Sered’s obstruction.
3. Finds missing maintenance line.
4. Seeks Brin Hesse to confirm the off-schedule route.
5. Returns with leverage.
6. Unlocks hidden passage.
7. Finds Tovin and the copied proof.

Primary feel:
- bureaucratic mystery becoming physical truth

## Path 3 — Political containment path
1. Player goes early to official authority.
2. Receives a narrowed, sanitized explanation.
3. Is encouraged to close the case as routine disappearance.
4. Can either accept this partial story or continue investigating.
5. If they continue, the case becomes more politically charged but also offers stronger leverage later.

Primary feel:
- teaches that official clarity may be false clarity

## Scene Design Notes

## Opening conversation with Sered Marr
What it should accomplish:
- establish obstruction immediately
- suggest that Tovin’s existence is already unstable in the records
- create incentive to seek informal truth elsewhere

Good outcomes:
- denied full access
- given a partial roster copy
- warned not to spread rumor
- directed toward shrine maintenance as a deflection

## First meaningful conversation with Ila Venn
What it should accomplish:
- introduce lantern interpretation as a real line of investigation
- establish that the official timeline is wrong
- give one concrete follow-up lead

Good outcomes:
- learn outage timing issue
- gain access to inspect lantern bracket
- receive direction toward maintenance evidence

## Maintenance discovery scene
What it should accomplish:
- give physical proof of deliberate alteration
- connect lantern system to access routes
- reward Clue Mastery and Lantern Understanding

## Hidden chamber discovery scene
What it should accomplish:
- pay off the search for Tovin
- humanize the stakes
- shift the case from “what happened” to “what truth will survive”

## Resolution Decision Point

Once the player has enough evidence and locates Tovin or his proof, the case should pivot into a resolution choice.

### Resolution question
How should this truth be made real?

That means the player is not only deciding what happened.
They are deciding what enters the city’s accepted memory.

## Main resolution routes

### 1. Clean exposure
Requirements:
- strong evidence set
- enough access or leverage to force acknowledgement

Action:
- present proof publicly or to unavoidable authority in a way that cannot be quietly buried

Result:
- Tovin is restored administratively and physically protected
- one faction loses face
- district tension rises temporarily
- player gains Reputation, Access, and City Impact

Tradeoff:
- the city becomes more unstable in the short term because confidence is shaken

### 2. Quiet rescue, limited truth
Requirements:
- locate Tovin
- secure a safe route or ally

Action:
- extract Tovin and preserve only enough evidence to stop immediate harm

Result:
- Tovin survives
- the full institutional truth remains hidden
- fewer public consequences
- player gains Access and some Leverage, but less City Impact

Tradeoff:
- the broader manipulation network survives for later cases

### 3. Political bargain
Requirements:
- leverage over Sered Marr, Sister Calis, or official authority

Action:
- trade silence or partial silence for restoration, access, or future favor

Result:
- Tovin may be quietly restored or relocated
- the case closes without public scandal
- player gains Leverage significantly
- faction relations become more complex

Tradeoff:
- truth is managed, not fully revealed
- future distrust risk remains in the district

### 4. Partial failure / stabilization
Requirements:
- enough evidence to understand the mechanism, but not enough to recover Tovin cleanly or prove full guilt

Action:
- stabilize lantern conditions and stop the immediate distortion

Result:
- the district stops worsening
- some records can be corrected later
- Tovin may remain missing or unconfirmed
- case is marked partially solved

Tradeoff:
- the player solved the symptom more than the injustice

### 5. Failure / burial
Trigger examples:
- too much delay
- player accepts official false closure
- evidence is mishandled
- pressure rises beyond recovery

Result:
- Tovin is absorbed into civic uncertainty or moved beyond easy recovery
- records settle into a false official version
- district stability improves superficially while truth worsens underneath
- case is failed, but creates strong future fallout

## Outcome Taxonomy

### Solved cleanly
- Tovin found
- evidence preserved
- manipulation exposed
- district remembers the event accurately enough going forward

### Solved politically
- Tovin recovered or acknowledged
- truth limited or negotiated
- factions reposition quietly

### Stabilized but unresolved
- lantern distortion stopped
- broader cause not fully proven
- victim fate or public truth remains incomplete

### Buried
- city accepts false explanation
- immediate unrest avoided
- underlying system remains intact

### Failed
- Tovin not recovered in time or official truth hardens against reality
- case closes with consequences instead of closure

## Recovery Prompts for This Case

The case should support clear stuck-state guidance.

Examples of useful recovery outputs:
- “The strongest unresolved thread is still the mismatch between the outage timing and the official disappearance report.”
- “You have reason to suspect both the archive office and the lantern maintenance record, but no proof tying them together yet.”
- “If the lantern was altered deliberately, someone with maintenance access or ritual knowledge must be involved.”
- “You still do not know whether Tovin fled, was hidden, or was made absent inside the records themselves.”

## Failure and Redirect Design

The case should avoid hard dead ends.

### Example redirects
If Sered denies access:
- the player can inspect public postings
- seek informal witnesses
- pressure him later with evidence

If Ila Venn refuses deeper help:
- the player can still inspect physical evidence
- seek Brin Hesse
- increase trust and return later

If the player goes to authority too early:
- they receive a sanitized explanation
- pressure rises
- but they may gain a new political target or clue about what is being suppressed

## Fallout Design

The case must leave the city changed.

## Local fallout variables
- Old Quarter lantern stability
- archive office credibility
- shrine-circle confidence
- district rumor intensity
- player reputation in Old Quarter
- player leverage with records officials

## Example fallout outcomes

### After clean exposure
- archive access becomes easier for a time
- district rumor intensity spikes, then settles
- official trust declines, but public awareness increases
- future case hooks open around records manipulation

### After quiet rescue
- Tovin survives off the books
- archive remains formally stable
- shrine keeper trust increases
- a future case hook opens around hidden survivors of Missingness

### After political bargain
- the player gains a durable favor or secret
- one official remains compromised
- future cases may reference this silence

### After failure
- official records harden
- the district feels superficially calmer but more false
- later cases inherit stronger Missingness pressure and distrust

## Why This Case Fits the MVP

This case stays small enough to build and test, but rich enough to exercise the game’s main systems.
It requires only:
- one primary district
- one secondary authority touchpoint
- a handful of tracked NPCs
- one hidden route
- one missing-person arc
- one lantern alteration event
- one meaningful resolution choice

But it still demonstrates the core Lantern City fantasy:
reading people, places, records, and light in order to decide what truth can survive in a city built on unstable memory.

## Design Rules

1. The case should begin with a human problem, not abstract lore.
2. Lantern effects should clarify the mystery, not replace it with nonsense.
3. No single NPC should explain the full truth alone.
4. At least one clue should change meaning after another is found.
5. The player should be able to finish with incomplete evidence, but better evidence should improve the quality of resolution.
6. The final choice should be about public truth, not just factual truth.
7. Every ending should leave persistent fallout.
8. The player should never need to solve the interface before solving the case.

## Summary

The Missing Clerk is the reference MVP case for Lantern City.
It combines missing-person investigation, contradictory records, lantern tampering, and institutional ambiguity into one contained arc.

It is the first case the game should be built around because it proves that Lantern City can deliver:
- investigation
- atmosphere
- stateful NPC play
- clue revision
- lantern-linked mystery
- consequential resolution
- lasting city change
