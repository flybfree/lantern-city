# Lantern City — District Spec: Salt Barrens

## District Summary

The Salt Barrens is the city's abandoned industrial outer district.
It is a district of decommissioned infrastructure, absence of official authority, and physical remnants of the city's older lantern network.
The lanterns here are extinguished. No one maintains them.
People and things persist here that the rest of the city has either forgotten or prefers not to acknowledge.

In play, the Salt Barrens should feel like:
- abandoned function rather than destroyed order
- physical evidence preserved by absence of interference
- knowledge held by people the city has stopped watching
- a place where the official record ends and material reality continues
- the city's margin, where what survives is what no one wanted to keep

## District Identity

### Public identity
The Salt Barrens is the district the city does not talk about.
It is known to exist — everyone who works in industrial or transit logistics has interacted with its edge.
But official maps do not detail it, and civic institutions do not maintain a presence there.
It is not a safe place by civic definition. It is uncontrolled.

### Lived identity
To salvage workers, scavengers, and those who have been pushed out of other districts, the Barrens is:
- survivable with local knowledge
- rich in discarded materials
- free from official oversight, which means free from official protection too
- a place where old lantern equipment goes when it is decommissioned
- where you find things the city removed from its own record

### Mechanical identity
In play, the Salt Barrens should specialize in:
- physical evidence from decommissioned or discarded sources
- information held by people operating outside official structures
- lantern history — the Barrens is a physical archive of the city's lantern past
- access through environmental knowledge rather than social standing
- clues that reveal what the city chose to remove and where it put it

## Governing Powers

The Salt Barrens is formally uncontrolled.

### Practical authority
- No governing body claims formal authority
- Salvage crews operate by informal territorial agreement
- Persin Loke and similar long-term workers hold practical knowledge of the district's layout and history

### Faction profile
- Memory Keepers: minimal influence (0.08) — theoretically interested in the physical lantern archive, but not operationally present
- Council of Lights: minimal influence (0.12) — responsible for the decommissioning process that created the Lantern Graveyard, but no longer maintaining any presence
- Neither faction operates in the Barrens. Their influence is historical, not active.

## Local Economy

The Salt Barrens has a salvage economy.

### Dominant economic functions
- scavenging and salvage of decommissioned materials
- informal material brokering to Docks and Market Spires contacts
- physical maintenance of salvage equipment

### Everyday workers
- salvage workers and scavengers
- informal brokers who move material out of the district
- occasional researchers or investigators from other districts — rare, and noted

## Social Rules and Norms

The Salt Barrens has a stripped-down social grammar.

### Core social norms
- presence here means you have a reason to be here — it is asked, not assumed
- official credentials carry no weight; practical utility does
- information is given in exchange for information or trust, not authority
- violence is not common, but warning lines are real
- the Barrens does not forget who came and why

### Player-facing effect
This district rewards:
- direct and honest statements of purpose
- demonstrated practical knowledge of what the player is looking for
- patience with indirect routes to trust
- treating Persin Loke and similar figures as the authorities they are in this space

And punishes:
- invoking civic authority or faction backing
- being vague about purpose
- treating the district as a resource to be extracted from
- underestimating the knowledge held by long-term workers here

## Lantern Profile

### Baseline district lantern condition
- extinguished throughout most of the district

### What this means in play
- testimony is maximally unreliable in most of the district
- physical evidence is the primary reliable clue source
- the extinguished condition is not suppressable — it is just the condition of the place
- what the lanterns looked like, and why they were decommissioned, is itself evidence

### Signature lantern feel
The Salt Barrens' light should feel:
- absent and cold rather than dim or flickering
- replaced by practical work light — lanterns workers carry rather than civic installations
- the dead casings in the Lantern Graveyard as physical evidence of the city's lantern history
- the extinguishment as decision, not neglect

### Social effects of the district lantern state
- no civic lantern effects operate here
- physical observation is primary
- the extinguished condition means the district is largely outside the missingness system — things here are preserved by absence rather than distorted by instability
- the city's lantern politics are legible here in physical form

## Hidden Problem

### Surface version
The Salt Barrens is simply abandoned — a consequence of industrial decline and decommissioning decisions.
The lanterns went out and no one came back to maintain them.

### Real district problem
The decommissioning process that created the Lantern Graveyard was not neutral.
Specific lantern equipment was moved here to remove it from the civic record.
Some of it contains evidence of what those lanterns were doing before decommissioning.
The Barrens is a physical archive of things the Council of Lights and others removed from official inventory.

### Why this matters
The Salt Barrens teaches the player that the city's margins are not empty.
They are storage for what the city decided not to keep officially.
The district is where physical evidence of city-level decisions survives precisely because no one maintained official attention here.

## Playable Locations

### 1. Abandoned Works

**Role:** district entry, industrial atmosphere, ambient decommissioning evidence
**Function:** First contact with the district's physical and sensory identity. Collapsed infrastructure, stripped lantern mounts, and the texture of deliberate absence.
**Scene objects:** collapsed beam stack, salt-crusted floor, stripped lantern mount
**Clue utility:** good for physical evidence of past industrial activity, observation of decommissioning patterns

### 2. Salvage Yard

**Role:** primary social space, informant access, knowledge of district layout
**Function:** Where Persin Loke works. Knowledge of what has been moved through, what persists, and the Barrens' history. Trust-gated access to deeper district knowledge.
**Key NPC:** Persin Loke (npc_salvage_worker)
**Scene objects:** sorted scrap pile, broken glass heap, hand-drawn boundary marker
**Clue utility:** good for testimony about material movement, knowledge of what decommissioned equipment contains, access paths to restricted areas

### 3. Lantern Graveyard (hidden)

**Role:** physical evidence archive, lantern history site, deep case breakthrough location
**Function:** Where decommissioned lantern equipment is physically stored. The material record of city-level lantern decisions. Physical evidence that contradicts official decommissioning narratives.
**Scene objects:** rows of dead lantern casings, corroded bracket pile, unlit ceremonial post
**Unlock conditions:** Persin Loke's trust, knowledge of the salvage yard layout, or physical inspection leading into the graveyard site
**Clue utility:** good for decommissioned lantern casings with evidence of pre-removal activity, physical documentation of what was removed from civic inventory, lantern history that connects to city-level cases

## NPC Distribution

### District anchor NPCs
- Persin Loke at Salvage Yard — informant, territorial, deeply knowledgeable about the district's physical history

### Ambient NPC types
- salvage workers with route knowledge but limited trust in outsiders
- occasional material brokers passing through
- no civic or faction NPC presence

## Rumor Pool

- "Nobody comes to the Barrens without a reason. The question is whether they say what it is."
- "The graveyard has more than dead casings. Some of them were pulled before they stopped working."
- "The Council decommissioned this district. They did not explain which parts they were keeping track of."
- "Persin knows what has been moved. He has been watching this yard longer than most people have been watching anything."
- "The Barrens is outside missingness. Things stay here the way they were when they arrived."
- "Extinguished lanterns are easier to read than lit ones. There is no interference."

## Typical Case Patterns in This District

The Salt Barrens should naturally support cases involving:
- decommissioned lantern equipment as physical evidence of city-level manipulation
- informal material networks connecting to Docks or Market Spires cases
- individuals who have been removed from the official city — living in or passing through the margins
- Council of Lights decisions about what gets decommissioned and why
- physical archives of things the city officially removed
- cases that require reaching outside civic authority structures entirely

## Distinctive Gameplay Pressures

### 1. Authority absence pressure
No civic structures function here. The player must operate without institutional backing.

### 2. Physical evidence primacy
With no reliable testimony and no documentary infrastructure, physical evidence is all that works.

### 3. Local knowledge pressure
Persin Loke and similar figures hold knowledge that cannot be obtained any other way.
Trust here is earned slowly or not at all.

### 4. Connection pressure
What the player finds here only matters if they can connect it to cases in other districts.
The Barrens' evidence is powerful but requires the player to carry it back into official space.

## Design Rules

1. The Salt Barrens should feel abandoned, not destroyed — deliberate, not chaotic.
2. Physical evidence is the primary mechanic here; testimony is secondary and limited.
3. The extinguished lanterns are history, not just atmosphere — they tell the story of what was removed.
4. Persin Loke is the only social anchor; access to the graveyard goes through him.
5. The district should connect outward to city-level cases that require physical evidence unavailable elsewhere.
6. The Barrens is outside the missingness system — things here are preserved by neglect, which is its own kind of reliability.
