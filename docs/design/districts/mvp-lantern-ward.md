# Lantern City — MVP District Spec: Lantern Ward

## Purpose

This document defines Lantern Ward as the secondary MVP district.
It serves as the official and ceremonial counterweight to the Old Quarter.

The Lantern Ward should support:
- the secondary district needs of The Missing Clerk case
- official lantern authority
- procedural legitimacy and denial
- political pressure around public truth
- a visible contrast with the Old Quarter’s dim uncertainty
- one or two decisive support scenes rather than a large exploration load

For the MVP, Lantern Ward does not need to be as deep as the Old Quarter.
But it does need to be clear, memorable, and mechanically distinct.

## District Summary

Lantern Ward is the ceremonial and administrative center of the city’s light culture.
Its streets are straighter, brighter, and more deliberately ordered than most of Lantern City.
Where the Old Quarter feels layered and inherited, Lantern Ward feels curated and maintained.
It presents light as legitimacy.

In play, Lantern Ward should feel like:
- official brightness
- managed certainty
- civic ritual as social control
- public order backed by selective access
- a place where the city decides which version of stability it can display

## Role in the MVP

Lantern Ward should provide what the Old Quarter cannot:
- formal channels
- official narrative pressure
- visible lantern regulation authority
- a route to procedural legitimacy
- a route to political containment

For The Missing Clerk case, this district should let the player:
- seek an official reading of the disturbance
- request or challenge formal authority
- meet or pressure higher-status figures such as Sister Calis or a lantern official
- decide whether to expose, negotiate, or soften the truth

### MVP scope rule
The Lantern Ward should be narrower than the Old Quarter.
It is a support district, not the core exploration district.
A few strong locations are enough if they are mechanically clear.

## District Identity

### Public identity
Lantern Ward is where the city performs confidence.
Public lantern standards, ceremonial lightings, regulatory offices, and approved civic pageantry all gather here.
If the city wants to look coherent, it does it in Lantern Ward.

### Lived identity
To workers and insiders, Lantern Ward is where:
- compliance is treated as virtue
- public calm matters more than private certainty
- the brightest streets hide the most carefully managed wording
- access is smoother if you belong and harder if you ask the wrong question plainly

### Mechanical identity
In play, Lantern Ward should specialize in:
- official answers that are incomplete but influential
- public order and reputational pressure
- access through legitimacy rather than secrecy
- strong contrast between visible clarity and hidden management
- resolution-stage decisions about what truth becomes formal truth

## Governing Powers

### Primary authority
- Council of Lights

They control:
- official lantern regulation
- maintenance certification standards
- public lantern law
- ceremonial approvals
- visible light infrastructure legitimacy

### Strong secondary influence
- senior Shrine Circle representatives and ritual liaisons

They influence:
- interpretation of unusual lantern behavior
- ceremonial framing of disturbances
- whether an event is treated as technical, spiritual, or politically sensitive

### Supporting civic authority
- watch officers, registrars, and administrative clerks

They influence:
- permits
- complaints
- formal complaint closure
- which investigation paths receive institutional standing

## Local Economy

Lantern Ward should feel less commercial than Market Spires and less archival than the Old Quarter.
Its economy is based on administration, ceremonial labor, and prestige service.

### Dominant economic functions
- lantern regulation
- compliance review
- ceremonial maintenance
- civic administration
- public-facing ritual events
- status services tied to official recognition

### Everyday workers in the district
- lamp inspectors
- ceremonial attendants
- clerks
- orderlies
- watch staff
- permit runners
- approved contractors
- ritual aides

### Economic pressure in the MVP
The district depends on public confidence in the city’s light system.
That means any proof of manipulation is dangerous here, not because it is strange, but because it threatens legitimacy.

## Social Rules and Norms

Lantern Ward should have a different social grammar from the Old Quarter.
It is less hesitant, but more managed.

### Core social norms
- appear composed
- use proper channels when possible
- do not embarrass authority in public unless prepared for the consequences
- speak in terms of compliance, safety, and order
- treat ritual and regulation as mutually reinforcing, not opposed
- let status shape tempo, access, and tone

### Player-facing effect
The district rewards:
- procedural language
- evidence framed in terms institutions can act on
- public restraint
- strategic timing
- knowing when to escalate formally

The district punishes:
- improvised accusation without formal support
- trying to force intimacy from status-bound NPCs
- confusing brightness with honesty
- assuming public access equals real access

### District mood rule
Lantern Ward should feel controlled even when under pressure.
Its version of tension is not whispering uncertainty, but polished containment.

## Lantern Profile

Lantern Ward should teach the player the other side of lantern power.
In the Old Quarter, lantern instability reveals uncertainty.
In Lantern Ward, stable light can reinforce sanctioned reality.

### Baseline district lantern condition
- bright and tightly regulated

### What this means in play
- public navigation is easy
- people seem more confident in official language
- the district appears orderly and trustworthy
- conflicting accounts are less tolerated, but not necessarily less real
- authority can use bright, stable conditions to frame its version of events as the reasonable one

### Signature lantern feel
The light here should feel:
- clean
- deliberate
- ceremonial
- watchful
- socially legible

### Social effects of the district lantern state
- people speak more decisively in public settings
- officials are harder to unsettle with rumor alone
- reputation matters more visibly
- deference and presentation affect outcomes strongly

### Mechanical lantern effects for the MVP
The district should demonstrate that bright lantern conditions can:
- improve confidence without guaranteeing truth
- strengthen official testimony weight
- make institutions more resilient against vague accusation
- reward the player for bringing stronger evidence rather than softer suspicion

## Hidden Problem

### Surface version
Lantern Ward is the city’s most orderly district and the best place to get a clean official answer.

### Real district problem
Its clarity is partly performative.
The district is built to absorb anomalies into acceptable narratives whenever possible.
Its institutions are good at deciding what may be acknowledged without harming confidence.

### Why this matters
The hidden problem of Lantern Ward is not uncertainty.
It is over-managed certainty.
This makes it the correct secondary district for The Missing Clerk case, because the player needs to see how truth can be neutralized by order.

## Active MVP Problems

At the start of the MVP, Lantern Ward should contain these quiet tensions:
- pressure to classify disturbances as isolated incidents
- institutional reluctance to admit manipulable lantern systems
- rivalry between regulatory authority and ritual interpretation
- concern that public scandal in the Old Quarter could spread distrust citywide

These should remain mostly background until the player brings evidence into the district.

## District Entry Experience

The first arrival in Lantern Ward should immediately contrast with the Old Quarter.

### What the player should notice on entry
- straighter streets
- cleaner paving
- stronger and more even light
- civic banners, marked posts, or formal insignia
- clearer lines of movement
- more visible hierarchy in clothing, posture, and access
- a mood of official calm that feels maintained rather than natural

### Entry text design rule
The district entry should imply competence and reassurance while suggesting that reassurance is part of the district’s job.

## Playable MVP Locations

Lantern Ward only needs a compact location set for the MVP.
Each location should justify the district’s inclusion in the case.

## 1. Office of Lantern Regulation

### Role
- primary official contact point
- procedural authority location
- source of formal interpretation or deflection

### Function in play
This is where the player can seek:
- official maintenance records
- incident framing
- a compliance-based explanation
- formal acknowledgement or denial

### Sensory identity
- polished counters
- regulated queues
- precise signage
- bright lamp arrays
- clean paper and metal fittings rather than damp archives

### Likely interactions
- request maintenance confirmation
- challenge a sanitized report
- ask what evidence would force action
- seek formal access or review
- encounter administrative narrowing

### Clue utility
Good place for:
- evidence of official framing
- confirmation of what was not logged properly
- procedural requirements for a clean exposure route

## 2. Ceremonial Walk / Processional Lightway

### Role
- transition and social-pressure space
- public face of lantern order
- place where the district demonstrates itself

### Function in play
This location should communicate the district’s ideology through environment.
It can host:
- overheard official sentiment
- public notices
- ceremonial preparations
- a chance encounter with higher-status figures

### Sensory identity
- evenly spaced lamps
- reflective stone
- banners, bells, or marked poles
- a sense that movement here is observed

### Likely interactions
- observe public notices
- overhear how the disturbance is being framed
- intercept or encounter a district official
- decide whether to make a public approach or stay discreet

## 3. Shrine Liaison Hall or Ritual Annex

### Role
- meeting point between ritual and regulation
- access node for Sister Calis or another informed authority
- negotiation space

### Function in play
This is where the player can confront the fact that ritual knowledge and official order are not separate systems.
It should support:
- partial truth conversations
- sanctioned explanation versus real explanation
- political bargaining

### Sensory identity
- cleaner than Shrine Lane but less intimate
- polished stone, controlled incense, regulated ceremonial objects
- warm but disciplined lighting

### Likely interactions
- speak to Sister Calis
- ask whether the alteration was deliberate
- test what the district already knows
- receive a containment offer or partial acknowledgement

### Clue utility
Good place for:
- confirmation that the alteration required knowledge
- suggestion that exposure and stability are now in tension
- bargain path unlocks

## 4. Civic Watch Desk / Complaint Office

### Role
- formal closure pressure point
- route to Watcher Pell or equivalent official
- procedural legitimacy gate

### Function in play
This location should make clear how cases are administratively ended.
It supports:
- false closure pressure
- formal reopening leverage
- official certification of a public outcome

### Sensory identity
- orderly benches
- complaint ledgers
- stamped forms
- watch officers who prefer manageable narratives

### Likely interactions
- ask how the case is currently classified
- challenge an incomplete close
- learn what proof is required
- secure formal support for exposure if enough evidence exists

## 5. Records Compliance Alcove or Authorized Review Room

### Role
- optional restricted support interior
- late-case confirmation space

### Function in play
This small location can serve as a late-stage support room where the player:
- reviews an official maintenance or incident extract
- compares public and internal framing
- gains one final piece of institutional leverage

This location does not need to be large.
Its purpose is precision, not exploration depth.

## District Movement Model

Lantern Ward should be easier to navigate than the Old Quarter.
That ease should be part of its identity.

### Suggested movement structure
- Office of Lantern Regulation as main anchor
- Ceremonial Walk as transition/public pressure space
- Shrine Liaison Hall as informed political node
- Civic Watch Desk as procedural closure node
- optional review room as restricted support interior

### Movement rule
Travel in Lantern Ward should usually feel direct.
Its friction comes from rank, permission, and social visibility, not from confusing layout.

## Access Patterns

The district should teach a different kind of access.
In the Old Quarter, access is buried behind trust, caution, and hidden routes.
In Lantern Ward, access is stratified by legitimacy.

### Public access
Available early:
- Ceremonial Walk
- public counters and waiting areas
- posted notices and formal announcements

### Restricted access
Requires:
- appointment
- recommendation
- procedural justification
- evidence that makes refusal costly

Relevant interiors:
- deeper regulation office access
- authorized review room
- private consultation with Sister Calis or another senior figure

### Socially restricted access
Some conversations are technically public but practically closed unless the player has:
- reputation
- leverage
- formal standing
- strong evidence

### Design goal
The player should feel that this district has many doors that are openly visible but not equally open.

## NPC Distribution in the District

Lantern Ward should be lighter on cast density than the Old Quarter, but strong in function.

### Primary case-linked NPC presence
- Sister Calis as ritual-authority voice
- Watcher Pell at watch or complaint office
- a lantern official, clerk, or compliance function through regulation spaces

### Visiting or conditional presence
- Sered Marr might send paperwork, warnings, or requests through this district
- player may arrive carrying evidence gathered in the Old Quarter

### Ambient/local NPC types
- lantern clerks
- ceremonial aides
- watch officers
- petitioners seeking official certification
- citizens who trust public order more than private rumor

## Rumor Pool

Lantern Ward rumors should sound less like rumors and more like polished public interpretation.

### Good rumor lines for the MVP
- “If there were a true systems breach, it would already have been contained.”
- “People confuse irregular memory with poor record discipline every season.”
- “The city cannot function if every lamp problem becomes a scandal.”
- “What the Old Quarter calls mystery is often simply unmanaged procedure.”
- “If the council acknowledges one manipulated field publicly, every district will invent three more.”

### Rumor design rule
Rumors in Lantern Ward should often sound plausible, disciplined, and incomplete rather than wild.
They should model sanctioned interpretation.

## Typical Case Patterns in This District

Lantern Ward should naturally support cases involving:
- official denial of emerging anomalies
- public ritual and private compromise
- legitimacy disputes
- compliance fraud or selective enforcement
- clean resolutions that hide ugly causes
- political bargains over what becomes public truth

## Distinctive Gameplay Pressures

Lantern Ward should pressure the player differently from the Old Quarter.

### 1. Legitimacy pressure
The player must think about what kind of evidence institutions will accept or refuse.

### 2. Reputation pressure
How the player presents themselves matters more here.
Public embarrassment can close doors as easily as open them.

### 3. Containment pressure
NPCs here often want resolution without revelation.
That makes this district central to political or quiet endings.

### 4. Official-confidence pressure
The player may be tempted to believe a cleaner explanation because it is cleaner, not because it is truer.

## District Feedback and State Labels

The district should expose a few readable state labels.

### Recommended visible district labels
- Lantern condition: bright / regulated / publicly stabilized
- Administrative posture: open / cautious / defensive
- Public confidence: steady / strained / managed
- Official tolerance: routine / narrowed / politically sensitive

These labels should help the player read how much room remains for direct pressure versus careful negotiation.

## Case-Specific District State Changes

The Missing Clerk should be able to alter Lantern Ward as follows.

### Before strong evidence arrives
- Lantern condition: bright
- Administrative posture: open but dismissive
- Public confidence: steady
- Official tolerance: routine

### After the player brings serious contradiction
- Lantern condition: bright but scrutinized
- Administrative posture: cautious
- Public confidence: strained
- Official tolerance: narrowed

### After clean exposure
- Lantern condition: publicly stabilized
- Administrative posture: defensive, then reform-minded depending on aftermath
- Public confidence: shaken but active
- Official tolerance: politically sensitive

### After political bargain
- Lantern condition: unchanged publicly
- Administrative posture: closed but cooperative in select channels
- Public confidence: steady on the surface
- Official tolerance: selective and watchful

### After failure or burial
- Lantern condition: officially affirmed
- Administrative posture: satisfied
- Public confidence: preserved publicly
- Official tolerance: colder toward future anomaly claims

## How the District Teaches the Game

Lantern Ward should teach the player that:
- official clarity is not the same as truth
- bright systems can normalize selective reality
- evidence must sometimes be translated into institutional language to matter publicly
- some victories come through exposure, others through managed pressure, and others through compromise
- the city’s light is both infrastructure and narrative control

## Interaction Model Hooks

Lantern Ward should work well with the hybrid interaction model in a different register from the Old Quarter.

### It should support
- structured procedural questions
- evidence-presentation scenes
- escalation and de-escalation choices
- public versus private approach decisions
- strong case-board use around outcome selection

### Good recovery outputs in this district
- “You have enough suspicion to concern them, but not enough proof to force them.”
- “This district responds better to documented contradiction than to intuition.”
- “If you want formal action, you need evidence that survives administrative review.”
- “Some people here may help you quietly only if public exposure becomes too dangerous to ignore.”

## Relationship to The Missing Clerk Case

Lantern Ward is where The Missing Clerk case broadens from a local mystery into a civic decision.
It is the district that asks:
- what can be acknowledged officially?
- what will be denied for the sake of calm?
- which institutions can be forced to act?
- what kind of ending the player wants to pursue?

Without this district, the case can still be solved factually.
With this district, it can be resolved politically.

## Authoring Notes for Future Expansion

If expanded later, Lantern Ward could support:
- ceremonial sabotage cases
- public-light legitimacy disputes
- council faction rivalries
- ritual law conflicts
- staged reforms that conceal deeper continuity problems

But for the MVP, none of that is necessary.
The district only needs to carry its support function clearly.

## MVP Build Checklist for This District

A playable MVP version of Lantern Ward should include:
- one district entry summary
- 3 to 4 interactable locations
- one official contact point
- one ritual-authority contact point
- one complaint or watch node
- one public transition space
- one district state object
- one official-posture state label
- one visible change in administrative stance based on the case outcome

## Design Rules

1. Lantern Ward should feel bright, orderly, and curated.
2. Its clarity should be socially real but epistemically incomplete.
3. It should pressure the player through legitimacy, not confusion.
4. It should provide a route to procedural action and a route to political containment.
5. It should contrast clearly with the Old Quarter in movement, tone, and access logic.
6. Public calm should be one of its main currencies.
7. Evidence should matter here, but presentation should matter too.
8. The district should make the player think about what kind of truth they want to make public.

## Summary

Lantern Ward is the MVP’s secondary district of official light.
It complements the Old Quarter by showing the institutional face of the lantern system: brighter, cleaner, more ordered, and more capable of turning uncertainty into acceptable public narrative.

If the Old Quarter teaches the player how to find the truth, Lantern Ward teaches them what it costs to make that truth official.
