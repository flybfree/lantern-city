# Lantern City — District Spec: The Docks

## District Summary

The Docks is the city's port and transit edge.
It is a district of movement, irregular record-keeping, and informal authority.
Cargo arrives and disappears. Names get swapped for route numbers.
The official lantern grid flickers here in ways that the harbormaster's office does not publicly acknowledge.

In play, The Docks should feel like:
- transient presence rather than permanent residence
- informal authority alongside official structures
- physical evidence over documentation
- routes that open and close based on who you know
- a place where continuity is commercially inconvenient

## District Identity

### Public identity
The Docks is where the city touches the outside world.
It is treated as a working district — necessary, a little rough, loosely supervised compared to the civic interior.
Institutions deal with it at arm's length unless something disrupts trade.

### Lived identity
To workers and regulars, The Docks is a district where:
- people move through without being tracked
- the flickering lanterns are a known condition, not a scandal
- informal arrangements sit alongside official manifests
- cargo movement creates social cover for other movement
- the harbormaster's authority is real but bounded by practical limits

### Mechanical identity
In play, The Docks should specialize in:
- physical and transit-based evidence
- informant networks
- access based on familiarity rather than formal permission
- clue discovery through presence and observation, not documentation
- lantern effects that blur identity and route rather than distorting memory

## Governing Powers

The Docks operates under overlapping practical authority.

### Primary practical authority
- Harbormaster (Harrow Selt) at the administrative level
- informal crew and hauler networks at the ground level

### Secondary official authority
- Council of Lights with moderate influence (0.45) — primarily concerned with lantern maintenance at the port edge and transit toll legitimacy
- The dockmaster functions as the point of contact for both, frequently playing them off each other

### Faction profile
- Memory Keepers: low influence (0.20) — interested in cargo manifests as records, but not structurally present
- Council of Lights: moderate influence (0.45) — maintain the lantern grid at the pier level, increasingly concerned about the flickering condition

## Local Economy

The Docks is a district of transit and handling.

### Dominant economic functions
- cargo handling and storage
- berthing and freight registration
- crew and hauler employment
- informal brokering of undocumented movement
- lantern maintenance contracts along the waterfront

### Everyday workers
- haulers and cargo handlers
- dock clerks and manifest checkers
- maintenance crews
- route runners and messengers
- unofficial brokers and transit fixers

## Social Rules and Norms

The Docks has a distinct social grammar shaped by transit culture.

### Core social norms
- presence is provisional — people pass through, not settle
- reliability matters more than rank
- specificity is suspicious — casual questions travel better than pointed ones
- who brought you matters as much as why you are here
- official requests should go through official channels; everything else happens elsewhere

### Player-facing effect
This district rewards:
- arriving with a connection or a referral
- asking practical questions rather than investigative ones
- observing physical space carefully
- building familiarity before asking about irregular activity

And punishes:
- civic formality without Docks context
- confronting informants directly
- assuming the harbormaster will be an ally
- treating the flickering lanterns as something anyone will acknowledge officially

## Lantern Profile

### Baseline district lantern condition
- flickering, with irregular pockets near older pier sections and cargo holding areas

### What this means in play
- testimony from witnesses in poorly-lit areas is less reliable
- physical evidence at inspected locations becomes more valuable than verbal accounts
- routes between lit and dark pier sections can feel uncertain or deliberately unclear
- the condition is known, managed, and not officially a problem

### Signature lantern feel
The Docks' light should feel:
- uneven and practical rather than ceremonial
- flickering in patterns that suggest missed maintenance rather than deliberate action
- enough to work by, not enough to trust fully
- occasionally extinguished around cargo holding spaces

### Social effects of the district lantern state
- workers know which spaces to avoid at night
- informal route knowledge carries real value
- uncertain light makes surveillance harder — in both directions
- the harbormaster uses maintenance scheduling to manage what is visible

## Hidden Problem

### Surface version
The Docks has a persistent lantern maintenance problem tied to irregular maintenance contracts and port-side exposure.

### Real district problem
Irregular cargo movement is partially enabled by selective lantern maintenance — specific areas go dark at predictable times.
The harbormaster knows. Some workers know.
The official record says it is a weather and age problem.

### Why this matters
The Docks teaches the player that absence of light is not always neglect.
Sometimes it is policy.

## Playable Locations

### 1. Pier Landing

**Role:** public entry point, transit anchor
**Function:** First contact with the district's social texture. Cargo movement, manifest board, and the unofficial rhythm of the dock.
**Scene objects:** mooring post, cargo manifest board, oil-stained rope coil
**Clue utility:** good for route observation, overheard transit information, timing-based physical evidence

### 2. Harbormaster Office

**Role:** official authority, access gating, administrative record access
**Function:** Where the player engages with Harrow Selt. Formal requests happen here. Contradiction between official manifest and physical evidence can surface here.
**Key NPC:** Harrow Selt (npc_dockmaster)
**Scene objects:** cargo register, berthing chart, damp inspection ledger
**Clue utility:** good for manifest discrepancies, missing or altered berthing records, access to restricted dock sections

### 3. Cargo Holding

**Role:** physical evidence site, informant space
**Function:** Where Maren Osk works. Physical cargo evidence, informal knowledge, and observation of what moves without being registered.
**Key NPC:** Maren Osk (npc_dock_hauler)
**Scene objects:** stacked crates, chalk lot markings, iron transit seal
**Clue utility:** good for unregistered cargo markings, physical transit evidence, testimony about irregular movement

### 4. Dock Workers Passage (hidden)

**Role:** hidden route, observation point, physical access breakthrough
**Function:** A service passage known to regular workers but not on official maps. Useful for physical evidence and informal access to restricted cargo areas.
**Scene objects:** unmarked door, soot-marked wall, discarded route slip
**Unlock conditions:** familiarity with dock workers, Maren Osk's trust, or route knowledge from physical inspection

## NPC Distribution

### District anchor NPCs
- Harrow Selt at Harbormaster Office — authority, moderate suspicion, protective of informal arrangements
- Maren Osk at Cargo Holding — informant, cautious, knows which routes are unofficial

### Ambient NPC types
- haulers moving cargo between pier and holding
- manifest clerks cross-checking paperwork
- maintenance workers doing timed lantern work
- route runners who know the passage network

## Rumor Pool

- "The lanterns here go dark on schedule. Everyone who works nights knows which schedule."
- "The manifest board shows what cleared. It does not show what moved before clearing."
- "Harrow knows what comes through. That is the job. Knowing does not mean stopping."
- "The passage behind holding has been unofficial longer than anyone here has been working."
- "Records at the docks are good for cargo weight. Everything else is approximate."

## Typical Case Patterns in This District

The Docks should naturally support cases involving:
- smuggled or unregistered goods tied to a larger network
- identity-based transit — people moving under cargo manifests
- lantern maintenance as deliberate route management
- authority figures protecting informal arrangements under official cover
- physical evidence that contradicts the paper record
- transit networks that connect multiple districts

## Distinctive Gameplay Pressures

### 1. Familiarity pressure
The player earns access through repeated presence and trusted contacts, not formal credentials.

### 2. Physical vs. documentary tension
Documents at the Docks are incomplete by design. Physical evidence matters more here.

### 3. Temporal pressure
Some access windows are timing-dependent — lantern maintenance, shift changes, cargo movement.

### 4. Authority ambiguity
The harbormaster has real power but uses it selectively. Civic authority from other districts does not translate directly here.

## Design Rules

1. The Docks should feel provisional — everything can be moved, changed, or denied.
2. Physical evidence should outweigh testimony here.
3. Familiarity unlocks more than formal permission.
4. The flickering lanterns are an active resource, not just atmosphere.
5. The harbormaster is not an antagonist — he is a pragmatist protecting his district's operating model.
6. The Docks should feel connected outward — routes from here can reach other districts and cases.
