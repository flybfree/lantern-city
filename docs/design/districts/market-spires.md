# Lantern City — District Spec: Market Spires

## District Summary

Market Spires is the city's commercial center.
It is a district of bright lanterns, visible transactions, and institutional opacity.
Trade here is official, tracked, and guild-mediated.
What moves freely is not information — it is price.

In play, Market Spires should feel like:
- organized commerce with structured gatekeeping
- surface legitimacy covering information asymmetry
- guilds as social infrastructure and power filter
- records that are maintained precisely because they can be selectively shared
- a district that runs well for people who know how it runs

## District Identity

### Public identity
Market Spires is where the city's commercial life is officially concentrated.
It is the most visibly functional-looking district — bright, ordered, and transaction-focused.
Visitors see a well-run market economy.
What they see is accurate, as far as it goes.

### Lived identity
To traders, guild members, and clerks, Market Spires is a district where:
- access to information is priced into every negotiation
- guild standing determines what you can know and when
- the bright lanterns are maintained because reputation requires them
- records are used as leverage before they are used as evidence
- commercial discretion is not dishonesty — it is professionalism

### Mechanical identity
In play, Market Spires should specialize in:
- information brokering and controlled disclosure
- access gating through commercial or guild standing
- documentary evidence with selective completeness
- NPCs who trade in knowledge as a commodity
- lantern stability used to signal legitimacy while obscuring detail

## Governing Powers

### Primary commercial authority
- Guild structures, coordinated through the Trade Guild Compliance system
- Cor Falt (guild steward) as the formal gateway between guild interests and outside inquirers

### Primary civic authority
- Council of Lights with significant influence (0.55) — responsible for maintaining the bright lantern grid that underpins market reputation

### Faction profile
- Memory Keepers: moderate influence (0.30) — interested in transaction records as a layer of civic memory
- Council of Lights: strong influence (0.55) — the bright lanterns here are a policy choice, not just infrastructure

## Local Economy

Market Spires is the city's dominant commercial district.

### Dominant economic functions
- wholesale and retail trade
- commercial record registration and certification
- guild administration and membership management
- transaction brokering
- information services tied to commercial decisions

### Everyday workers
- traders and merchants
- records clerks and certification staff
- guild compliance officers
- transaction brokers
- administrative staff managing commercial certifications

## Social Rules and Norms

Market Spires has a commercial social grammar.

### Core social norms
- everything has a price — including information
- guild standing is respected before civic rank
- indirection is professional, not evasive
- records are assets, not public resources
- a deal made publicly carries weight; a deal made privately carries risk
- transparency is performed, not practiced

### Player-facing effect
This district rewards:
- offering something in exchange for information
- framing inquiries in commercial terms
- working through proper guild channels when possible
- identifying who controls what kind of record

And punishes:
- civic authority claims that do not carry commercial weight here
- direct interrogation without standing
- assuming the bright lanterns indicate openness
- treating guild compliance as a bureaucratic obstacle rather than a power structure

## Lantern Profile

### Baseline district lantern condition
- bright, maintained deliberately as a commercial signal

### What this means in play
- testimony here is more reliable on the surface — NPCs are more confident
- but reliability can be performance rather than truth
- the bright condition creates a false ceiling on how much the player should trust corroboration
- physical evidence from secondary spaces (back offices, restricted areas) often contradicts the official surface

### Signature lantern feel
Market Spires' light should feel:
- deliberate and performative
- brighter than necessary for practical purposes
- a statement about the district's self-image
- revealing of surface, concealing of depth

### Social effects of the district lantern state
- the bright condition signals credibility to visitors and traders
- it makes uncertainty seem like the player's problem, not the district's
- witnesses here speak with more confidence than their knowledge justifies
- the gap between stated reliability and actual reliability is a core mechanical feature

## Hidden Problem

### Surface version
Market Spires appears well-run and commercially healthy.
Records are complete. Lanterns are bright. Transactions are registered.

### Real district problem
Commercial information here is controlled, not transparent.
Guild networks broker access to records in ways that benefit members and disadvantage outsiders.
The Records Office and Back Counter together constitute a two-tier information system:
one public face and one where actual leverage is managed.

### Why this matters
Market Spires teaches the player that bright light does not mean visible truth.
It teaches them to look for what the official record is designed to show — and what it is designed not to show.

## Playable Locations

### 1. Trade Floor

**Role:** public entry, ambient commercial activity, district atmosphere
**Function:** First contact with the district's social texture. Market rhythm, visible transaction culture, and the district's self-presentation.
**Scene objects:** merchant stall, price board, public lantern column
**Clue utility:** good for ambient testimony, overheard commercial disputes, observation of transaction patterns

### 2. Records Office

**Role:** primary records access point, information brokering space
**Function:** Where Vel Dassen works. Documentary evidence, commercial information with price attached, and controlled disclosure.
**Key NPC:** Vel Dassen (npc_records_broker)
**Scene objects:** transaction ledger, filing press, ink-stained counter
**Clue utility:** good for transaction records, commercial connections between districts, selective document access

### 3. Guild Hall

**Role:** institutional authority, access gating, guild political space
**Function:** Where Cor Falt operates. Guild standing determines access and information. Formal guild channels and their limits.
**Key NPC:** Cor Falt (npc_guild_steward)
**Scene objects:** charter display, guild seal press, members register
**Clue utility:** good for membership records, access history, guild-protected transactions

### 4. Back Counter (hidden)

**Role:** secondary information tier, leverage space, restricted evidence site
**Function:** The back office where genuinely sensitive records and commercial correspondence are handled. Access through trust, trade, or leverage.
**Scene objects:** unmarked ledger stack, curtained access door, sealed correspondence tray
**Unlock conditions:** Vel Dassen's trust, guild standing, or evidence leverage
**Clue utility:** good for sealed correspondence, records that contradict the public face, off-ledger transactions

## NPC Distribution

### District anchor NPCs
- Vel Dassen at Records Office — informant, commercially motivated, trades information for advantage
- Cor Falt at Guild Hall — gatekeeper, protective of guild interests, procedurally correct

### Ambient NPC types
- traders with complaints about competitor access
- certification clerks processing routine registrations
- guild members checking compliance status
- visitors from other districts trying to navigate commercial channels

## Rumor Pool

- "Vel Dassen knows what everyone paid for. What he tells you depends on what you offer."
- "The guild does not block access. It just routes it."
- "The bright lanterns here are policy. Ask the Council of Lights what they paid for the contract."
- "A transaction goes two ways. The record usually only captures one of them."
- "The back counter is where the real ledgers go. The front is for certification."
- "Commercial discretion means the information exists. It just has a holder."

## Typical Case Patterns in This District

Market Spires should naturally support cases involving:
- commercial fraud or falsified transaction records
- guild-protected individuals with connections to other districts
- information brokers with selective knowledge of larger networks
- Council of Lights commercial interests intersecting with civic authority
- trade routes as transit infrastructure for non-commercial movement
- bright-surface legitimacy concealing deep information asymmetry

## Distinctive Gameplay Pressures

### 1. Information price pressure
Almost everything the player needs has a holder who expects something in return.
Progress requires exchange, not just investigation.

### 2. Legitimacy surface pressure
The bright lanterns and well-maintained records make it easy to accept official accounts.
The district rewards skepticism of surfaces.

### 3. Guild access pressure
Guild standing gates access in ways that civic rank does not override.
The player must work through commercial or social channels.

### 4. Documentary reliability pressure
Records here are complete — but selectively complete.
The player must learn to identify what is missing from an apparently full document.

## Design Rules

1. Market Spires should feel functional on the surface and asymmetric underneath.
2. Information is a commodity here — the player should expect to trade.
3. Bright lanterns should create false confidence that the player learns to read critically.
4. Guild authority is not corrupt — it is self-interested. The distinction matters.
5. The back counter is not secret — it is just not for everyone.
6. Market Spires should connect to other districts through commercial and transit networks.
