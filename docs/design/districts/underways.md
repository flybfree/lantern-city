# Lantern City — District Spec: The Underways

## District Summary

The Underways is the city's buried infrastructure layer.
It is a district of maintenance conduits, junction chambers, and the physical substrate of the lantern grid.
Everything that the civic lantern system does above ground has a mechanical cause below it.
The Underways is where that causation lives.

In play, The Underways should feel like:
- physical truth rather than social truth
- the root system that feeds everything visible above
- a space where the city's official map ends and its real structure begins
- access earned through knowledge accumulated in other districts
- the place where what is happening in the city becomes mechanically legible

## District Identity

### Public identity
The Underways does not officially exist as a district.
Maintenance access to sub-street conduits is managed through Council of Lights permits.
The Shrine Circle technical corps has its own access protocols.
Neither authority publicizes the route network.
To most of the city, there is a below-street maintenance system. It is technical, unremarkable, and not a place.

### Lived identity
To maintenance workers, route wardens, and those who have found or been shown access:
- the Underways is the city seen from underneath
- what looks like district separation above ground is porous here — a warden can move between Old Quarter, Lantern Ward, and the Docks without using a single street
- the lanterns in the conduits are not civic lanterns — they are maintenance work lights, altered from their original function
- the altered condition is not a malfunction — it is the natural state of light that has been rerouted, modified, and repurposed over generations
- knowledge of the route network is one of the most durable forms of access in the city

### Mechanical identity
In play, the Underways should specialize in:
- physical evidence of how lantern manipulations are performed
- cross-district route access for cases that span multiple districts
- altered lantern conditions that produce uniquely unreliable testimony but uniquely clear physical evidence
- deep case breakthroughs — the Underways is where a multi-district case converges
- access to things that cannot be reached through any surface district alone

## How the District Is Discovered

The Underways is not entered through a standard district entry command.
It is discovered.

### Discovery conditions (any one sufficient)
- physical inspection of a maintenance passage (Service Passage, Dock Workers Passage) reveals a connected route
- an NPC with route knowledge (Brin Hesse, Persin Loke, or a generated route-linked NPC) discloses access
- a generated case requires cross-district physical evidence and routes the player through the Underways
- a high-enough lantern_understanding tier unlocks interpretation of structural clues found in other districts

Once discovered, the player can enter normally like any district.

### Player experience of discovery
The first time the player reaches an Underways access point, it should feel like a different register of the game opening — not a new area, but a new layer of the same city.

## Governing Powers

The Underways has competing partial authorities, none of which control it fully.

### Technical custodial authority
- Council of Lights, through maintenance permit structures
- They issue permits for access. They do not have full knowledge of the route network.
- Their 0.35 influence reflects custodial claim, not operational control.

### Practical route authority
- Shrine Circle technical corps, through generations of inherited route knowledge
- Memory Keepers, through access to structural records predating the Council's administration
- The Memory Keepers' 0.45 influence is structural — their archives contain the oldest route maps.

### Faction profile
- Memory Keepers: 0.45 — the route network predates the Council; Memory Keepers hold the oldest access documentation
- Council of Lights: 0.35 — official permit authority, but limited actual knowledge of the full system

Neither faction fully controls the Underways. The tension between them is readable here in physical form — competing route markers, different maintenance standards applied at different junction points.

## Local Economy

There is no commercial economy in the Underways.

### Functional presence
- maintenance workers on contracted route assignments
- Shrine technical corps workers on access rotations
- route wardens with long-term informal claim to specific sections
- occasional researchers, investigators, or anyone with a reason to be below street level

## Social Rules and Norms

The Underways has a stripped and functional social grammar.

### Core social norms
- you are here for a reason — state it plainly or do not state it at all
- route knowledge is the primary form of standing here
- official credentials from surface districts carry reduced weight below ground
- a route warden's word about their section is final
- what happens in a junction is between the people in the junction

### Player-facing effect
This district rewards:
- arriving with clues from other districts that establish why the player is here
- demonstrating prior knowledge of the route network
- respecting territorial claims on specific sections
- treating physical evidence carefully — the altered condition distorts memory but not objects

And punishes:
- surface authority claims without route-level standing
- treating the space as a shortcut without understanding what it is
- rushing past physical evidence to reach a destination

## Lantern Profile

### Baseline district lantern condition
- altered throughout

### What this means in play
- altered lanterns produce the most complex testimony distortion in the game
- witnesses here report events with inconsistent framing — not false, but structurally incomplete
- physical evidence is not distorted by the altered condition — it is often better preserved here than on the surface, because no one has been managing the scene
- the altered condition is the city's lantern history made literal — layers of modification, repurposing, and partial repair visible in a single fixture

### Signature lantern feel
The Underways' light should feel:
- functional but wrong — the right brightness for work, the wrong color or source for civic use
- warm in some sections, cold in others, depending on which era of modification applies
- present enough to work by, strange enough to unsettle testimony
- a record of every time someone rerouted or modified the grid, legible in the fixtures themselves

### Social effects of the district lantern state
- workers do not discuss the altered condition — it is simply the condition
- the distortion affects memory-adjacent testimony but not physical observation
- NPCs in the Underways give reliable physical descriptions and unreliable interpretive accounts
- the player should learn to separate what witnesses observed from what they concluded

## Hidden Problem

### Surface version
The Underways is a maintenance infrastructure district with limited access and a persistent altered lantern condition attributed to age and layered modifications.

### Real district problem
The route network is how cross-district lantern manipulations are performed.
The physical evidence of every significant lantern alteration in the city is findable in the Underways if you know where to look.
The junction chambers contain the physical interface points where above-ground lanterns are modified from below.
Several factions use the route network for access and movement that bypasses surface authority.

### Why this matters
The Underways is where the city's cases become structurally connected.
A player who reaches the Underways and reads it correctly understands the city differently — not just one case, but the system that makes multiple cases possible.

## Playable Locations

### 1. Junction Hall

**Role:** district entry anchor, route intersection, orientation space
**Function:** The main maintenance node where conduit routes from multiple districts converge. The player's first experience of the district's spatial logic and physical evidence density.
**Key NPC:** Renn Dour (npc_route_warden) — informal warden of this junction section
**Scene objects:** junction lantern array, route marker board, maintenance tool alcove, district branch seals
**Clue utility:** good for cross-district route evidence, physical evidence of recent access from multiple directions, route marker analysis

### 2. Conduit Run

**Role:** primary transit passage, cross-district movement route, physical evidence corridor
**Function:** The main maintenance passage connecting multiple district access points. Evidence of who uses the route and when — footprints, tool marks, material transfers.
**Scene objects:** worn stone conduit floor, rerouted lantern housing, transit mark series, access ladder rungs
**Clue utility:** good for movement evidence, evidence of specific district origins, physical material carried from or to other districts

### 3. Alteration Chamber (hidden)

**Role:** deep case breakthrough location, physical mechanism site
**Function:** The junction point where above-ground lantern modifications are physically performed. Physical evidence of specific alterations ties here to cases in surface districts.
**Scene objects:** exposed lantern conduit junction, modification tool set, fresh and old work marks, faction-specific maintenance notation
**Unlock conditions:** route knowledge from Renn Dour, physical evidence from surface districts pointing to sub-street activity, or high lantern_understanding tier
**Clue utility:** the Alteration Chamber is where physical evidence connects to specific surface district cases — heat scoring on brackets, missing maintenance lines, and lantern condition anomalies all have a corresponding physical record here

## NPC Distribution

### District anchor NPCs
- Renn Dour (npc_route_warden) at Junction Hall — informant, long-term route warden, territorial about the junction section, cautious about new arrivals but not hostile

### Ambient NPC types
- maintenance workers on contracted assignments
- Shrine technical corps rotations passing through
- no Council-affiliated NPCs below the permit level
- NPCs from other districts who know the route network (Brin Hesse can be found here; Persin Loke has route knowledge connecting to the Barrens)

## Rumor Pool (surface-level, heard in other districts)

- "There are wardens who have not been above ground in weeks. The routes have their own rhythm."
- "The maintenance permits go to the Council. The routes go somewhere older."
- "If something changed in the lanterns, the change happened below first."
- "The archive does not have the current route maps. Those exist in the junction markings."
- "What gets moved through the Underways does not touch a manifest anywhere."
- "There is a chamber where the old work and the new work both leave marks. It is not on the official maintenance plan."

## Typical Case Patterns in This District

The Underways should naturally support cases involving:
- multi-district lantern manipulation with physical evidence below ground
- cross-district movement that bypasses surface authority checkpoints
- faction competition for route access and physical infrastructure control
- specific lantern alterations tied to cases in Old Quarter, Lantern Ward, or The Docks
- physical evidence chains that begin in surface districts and resolve here
- individuals using the route network for access, concealment, or material movement

## Distinctive Gameplay Pressures

### 1. Discovery access pressure
The player must earn their way into the Underways through investigation in other districts.
It cannot be entered casually.

### 2. Physical evidence primacy with testimony limits
The altered condition makes witness accounts structurally incomplete.
Physical observation is the primary reliable mode here.

### 3. Cross-district connection pressure
Evidence found here only matters when connected to cases above ground.
The Underways is the place where cross-district cases become solvable.

### 4. Route knowledge pressure
Moving through the district without route knowledge is disorienting.
Renn Dour and other wardens gate deeper access.

## Connection to Other Districts

The Underways physically connects beneath:
- Old Quarter — access from Service Passage
- The Docks — access from Dock Workers Passage
- Salt Barrens — access from the Lantern Graveyard edge (unofficial, known to Persin Loke)
- Lantern Ward and Market Spires — access points exist but are more heavily permit-gated

This makes the Underways the only district with physical sub-routes to four other districts.
For case generation, the Underways should be available as a connecting district in multi-district cases.

## Design Rules

1. The Underways is discovered, not entered — the first access should feel like a revelation.
2. Physical evidence is primary; altered testimony is unreliable in specific and consistent ways.
3. Route knowledge is the district's social currency — respect it accordingly.
4. The Alteration Chamber is the most significant physical evidence site in the game for city-level cases.
5. The district should feel like the city seen from underneath — the same facts, structured differently.
6. Every surface district anomaly has a physical cause somewhere in the Underways.
