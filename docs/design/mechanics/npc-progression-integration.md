# Lantern City — NPC Interaction and Progression Integration

## Purpose

This document connects NPC conversations to the player’s progression tracks.
The goal is to make social interaction a real source of advancement, not just flavor.

## Core Idea

NPC conversations should feed progression directly.
When the player talks to someone meaningful, they should gain at least one of:
- Lantern Understanding
- Access
- Reputation
- Leverage
- City Impact
- Clue Mastery

## Track-by-Track Integration

### Lantern Understanding
NPCs increase this track when they:
- explain lantern behavior
- reveal who controls a lantern system
- show the difference between physical failure and ritual alteration
- describe how lantern effects reach across a district

Useful NPC types:
- shrine workers
- memory specialists
- lantern technicians
- old residents with long memory

### Access
NPCs increase this track when they:
- grant entry to a location
- provide credentials or introductions
- unlock a private meeting
- reveal a hidden route
- vouch for the player

Useful NPC types:
- gatekeepers
- officials
- faction members
- trusted locals

### Reputation
NPCs increase or decrease this track when they:
- spread word of the player’s actions
- judge the player’s reliability
- witness promises kept or broken
- influence how a district views the player

Useful NPC types:
- community figures
- faction representatives
- public witnesses
- gossip sources

### Leverage
NPCs generate leverage when they:
- reveal a secret
- admit wrongdoing
- describe a hidden motive
- owe the player a favor
- leave behind contradiction or proof

Useful NPC types:
- nervous insiders
- compromised officials
- rival agents
- people with something to lose

### City Impact
NPCs contribute to city impact when they:
- adapt their behavior because of the player
- relay the player’s reputation upward
- change faction plans in response to player action
- make the player’s presence matter in a larger system

Useful NPC types:
- faction leaders
- district organizers
- influential brokers
- recurring characters with memory and agency

### Clue Mastery
NPCs increase clue mastery when they:
- provide contradictory accounts
- teach the player how to interpret a district
- force the player to compare narratives
- reveal how the city hides information

Useful NPC types:
- informants
- historians
- investigators
- people who disagree with official records

## Conversation Outcomes That Should Advance Progress

An NPC interaction should count as progress if it does any of the following:
- reveals a new clue
- deepens a clue the player already had
- changes an NPC relationship state
- opens a new place or route
- changes what the player believes about lanterns
- exposes a contradiction in the city’s official story
- creates a usable favor, debt, or promise

## Conversation Outcomes That Should Not Count As Progress Alone

Some interactions are still valuable, but should not be treated as progression by themselves unless they connect to a larger state change.
Examples:
- pure flavor banter
- repeated filler dialogue
- restating known facts without new context
- generic “nice weather” exchanges

These can still enrich the world, but they should not inflate the player’s sense of advancement.

## Recommended Feedback Model

After meaningful dialogue, the game should summarize what changed.
Examples:
- “You learned that the lantern outage was not accidental.”
- “The foreman now trusts you enough to speak privately.”
- “The district now associates your name with the missing clerk.”
- “You gained a useful lead on lantern maintenance records.”

This makes progress visible and reinforces player agency.

## Design Rule

If a conversation meaningfully changes the game state, it should update at least one progression track.
If it does not change state, it should remain light, optional, and easy to leave.
