# Lantern City — MVP Faction Dossier: Memory Keepers

## Purpose

This document defines the Memory Keepers as an operational faction for the MVP.
It translates their high-level concept into a usable design profile that can support:
- district authority in the Old Quarter
- records-based pressure in The Missing Clerk case
- access gating and documentary legitimacy
- faction reaction to anomaly, scandal, and exposure
- future case reuse

For the MVP, the Memory Keepers should function as a faction that is not simply villainous or benevolent.
They should feel like custodians of continuity who are always in danger of becoming editors of reality.

## Faction Summary

The Memory Keepers are the city’s archivists, custodians of names, registrars of continuity, and stewards of civic record legitimacy.
Officially, they preserve what the city must remember.
Unofficially, they help decide which versions of memory can survive institutional life.

In play, they should feel like:
- procedural authority
- continuity as power
- calm language masking dangerous discretion
- protectors of civic memory who may also narrow it

## Public Role

The Memory Keepers publicly exist to:
- preserve records
- maintain civic continuity
- authenticate names, lines, and claims
- guard archives and registries
- prevent confusion, duplication, and historical drift

Their public self-image is necessary and respectable.
Most ordinary people believe that without them, inheritance, identity, and obligation would collapse into chaos.

## Private Logic

The faction’s real operating belief is not merely “preserve everything.”
It is:
- preserve what keeps the city legible
- contain anomalies before they become contagious
- prevent public uncertainty from breaking institutional trust
- decide when correction is safer than disclosure

This does not make them simple censors.
It makes them a faction that sees continuity itself as a civic resource that must be managed.

## Core Faction Fantasy

The Memory Keepers should feel like the faction of:
- sealed shelves
- careful corrections
- respectable omission
- orderly power
- dangerous discretion exercised in quiet rooms

They are one of the clearest examples in Lantern City of how the city can turn truth into administration.

## Primary Territory and District Footprint

### Primary district anchor
- Old Quarter

### Strong influence in
- archives
- registry halls
- legal record offices
- any district where names, ownership, or continuity need certification

### Secondary influence in the MVP
- formal records touchpoints that connect to Lantern Ward bureaucracy

### Practical district identity
In the Old Quarter, the Memory Keepers are the practical authority even when they are not the only authority.
They decide:
- who gets access
- which records are considered valid
- what discrepancies are “under review” rather than publicly acknowledged

## Resources and Assets

### Core assets
- archives and registry rooms
- clerks, registrars, and copyists
- document seals and authentication practices
- access permissions and procedural bottlenecks
- institutional credibility
- historical knowledge not available to the public

### Less visible assets
- quiet relationships with civic officials
- knowledge of old contradictions and buried disputes
- the ability to slow, narrow, or redirect a case through procedure
- the power to make a person spend time proving what should have been obvious

### Asset type in play
Their strongest resource is not force.
It is legitimacy.
They can make uncertainty official, delay recognition, and decide what counts as sufficiently proven.

## Methods

The Memory Keepers prefer to act through:
- access control
- document review
- procedural delay
- partial disclosure
- controlled correction
- reputational framing
- careful language that limits scope

They do not usually act through open intimidation unless backed into a corner.
Their ideal outcome is that the city accepts their narrowing of the issue as ordinary caution.

## Public Language Style

When speaking publicly or institutionally, Memory Keepers should sound:
- precise
- restrained
- formal
- careful not to overclaim
- subtly narrowing in scope

Typical phrasing patterns:
- “The matter is under review.”
- “The records are presently inconsistent.”
- “No certified conclusion has been reached.”
- “Premature conclusions would damage public trust.”
- “The discrepancy may be procedural rather than substantive.”

This language should feel useful, respectable, and dangerous.

## Internal Beliefs

A useful internal creed for the faction is:
- continuity is more fragile than the public understands
- people need records they can trust more than they need every raw truth at once
- uncontrolled ambiguity spreads faster than correction
- some truths must be phased into public life carefully or not at all

This is where the faction becomes morally interesting.
They can be sincerely trying to protect the city while still doing harm.

## Relationships with Other Factions

### Shrine Circles
Relationship:
- necessary, tense cooperation

Why:
- shrines understand lantern behavior that archives cannot ignore
- Memory Keepers distrust ritual opacity
- shrine actors distrust archival narrowing of strange events

MVP expression:
- blame shifting over whether the outage was negligence, anomaly, or manipulation
- fragile cooperation if the player handles both sides carefully

### Council of Lights
Relationship:
- institutional rivalry and mutual dependence

Why:
- the Council regulates official light
- the Memory Keepers regulate official continuity
- both claim they preserve civic order
- each dislikes being made dependent on the other’s explanation

MVP expression:
- both factions want containment, but for slightly different reasons
- each may privately blame the other for reputational damage

## Red Lines

The Memory Keepers become much more dangerous when:
- archive credibility is threatened publicly
- unauthorized record access becomes visible
- a discrepancy suggests broader records instability
- someone implies that names and claims can be administratively altered at will
- lower-ranking Keepers are exposed as having hidden or rewritten evidence

Crossing these lines may cause them to:
- shut down access
- formalize denial
- involve authority figures
- shift from cautious help to defensive containment

## What Success Looks Like for Them

A successful outcome for the Memory Keepers is:
- the discrepancy is contained
- public trust in records survives
- archive authority remains intact
- any correction appears orderly rather than scandal-driven
- responsibility is localized rather than systemic

Importantly, their ideal success is not always full truth.
It is stable continuity.

## What Failure Looks Like for Them

Failure for the Memory Keepers means:
- public recognition that records can be deliberately shaped
- visible contradiction between official copies
- exposure of internal suppression or selective access
- a player or rival faction proving that continuity has been managed dishonestly
- spillover distrust into other districts or institutions

## Role in The Missing Clerk Case

In the MVP case, the Memory Keepers should be the faction most associated with:
- denied access
- contradictory records
- controlled embarrassment
- documentary pressure
- the question of whether the city will officially remember Tovin Vale

### Important rule
The faction should not be reduced to “they erased him.”
The more interesting design is that different members want different responses:
- some want clean correction
- some want quiet containment
- some fear scandal more than injustice
- some may not know the whole truth

This preserves nuance and replay value.

## Example MVP Positions Within the Faction

### Sered Marr type
- practical containment
- access gatekeeping
- worried about scandal and career damage

### unseen superior or office logic
- wider concern about institutional trust
- preference for controlled correction if unavoidable

### lower clerical layer
- anxious about inconsistency
- likely to comply with procedure even when it feels wrong

## Player-Facing Behaviors

The player should experience the Memory Keepers as a faction that:
- can open the case if persuaded or cornered
- can close the case if mishandled
- rewards evidence and precision
- resists rumor and emotional appeal alone
- can become a procedural ally or a documentary obstacle

## Reaction Logic

### If the player is careful and evidence-driven
The faction becomes:
- cautious but workable
- willing to allow partial access
- more likely to admit limited irregularity

### If the player is loud without proof
The faction becomes:
- defensive
- dismissive
- procedurally obstructive
- more likely to involve watch or higher authority

### If the player proves systemic risk
The faction splits internally between:
- exposure for controlled repair
- containment for survival

That split is excellent drama for later expansion.

## What They Can Offer the Player

If engaged successfully, the Memory Keepers can provide:
- restricted document access
- credibility for a resolution
- official acknowledgement of a discrepancy
- names, timelines, or records unavailable elsewhere
- leverage through documented contradiction

## What They Can Deny the Player

If engaged poorly, they can deny:
- entry
- document review
- record legitimacy
- time
- confidence in the player’s claims

## Reputation Effects

Reputation with the Memory Keepers should rise when the player:
- demonstrates precision
- avoids reckless public embarrassment
- distinguishes error from manipulation
- helps restore continuity without pure chaos

Reputation should fall when the player:
- makes wild claims without evidence
- treats archives as decorative rather than authoritative terrain
- causes scandal with no plan for fallout

## Long-Term Faction Hook

For future cases, the Memory Keepers can anchor stories about:
- buried names
- altered inheritance lines
- civic amnesia framed as correction
- archive factions competing over what must survive
- bureaucratic mercy versus bureaucratic violence

## Design Rules

1. The Memory Keepers should feel necessary, not optional.
2. Their power should come from legitimacy and procedure, not brute force.
3. They should be capable of both preservation and harm using the same tools.
4. Internal variation matters; not every Keeper wants the same thing.
5. Their preferred weapon is narrowing the scope of reality until it becomes manageable.
6. They should make the player think carefully about the difference between truth and continuity.

## Summary

The Memory Keepers are the faction of civic memory under institutional pressure.
They preserve the city’s continuity, but that same power lets them edit what can be safely acknowledged.

In the MVP, they should be one of the central moral and procedural forces in The Missing Clerk case.
