# Lantern City — MVP Faction Dossier: Shrine Circles

## Purpose

This document defines the Shrine Circles as an operational MVP faction.
It gives them enough structure to support:
- local lantern maintenance authority in the Old Quarter
- ritual interpretation of anomalies
- nuanced cooperation and tension with secular institutions
- meaningful NPC behavior in The Missing Clerk case
- future expansion into broader lantern and belief systems

For the MVP, the Shrine Circles should feel like stewards of necessary knowledge whose opacity can protect the city or distort it.

## Faction Summary

The Shrine Circles are the keepers of local ritual practice, caretakers of shrines, interpreters of unusual lantern behavior, and custodians of the city’s intimate light customs.
They are closer to streets, habits, and lived atmosphere than the Council of Lights, and closer to lantern reality than the Memory Keepers.

In play, they should feel like:
- custodians of practical mystery
- local stabilizers
- interpreters of what official systems do not fully understand
- guardians of techniques they do not want fully exposed

## Public Role

The Shrine Circles publicly exist to:
- maintain shrines and local ritual practice
- tend lanterns where ritual care matters
- guide citizens through omen, loss, memory, and disturbance
- keep neighborhoods spiritually and socially coherent

Many ordinary people trust them in the way they trust weathered local expertise.
Even skeptical residents may still defer to them when lantern behavior turns strange.

## Private Logic

Internally, the Shrine Circles believe:
- not everything stable should be explained publicly
- ritual knowledge is powerful partly because it is bounded
- official regulation only understands part of the lantern system
- public panic can be worsened by truth delivered without context
- some forms of maintenance look irrational until you understand the system they are protecting

This makes them neither simple mystics nor pure political actors.
They are custodians of a partly hidden operational knowledge.

## Core Faction Fantasy

The Shrine Circles should feel like the faction of:
- warm glass in rain
- careful relighting
- whispered practical doctrine
- local knowledge with citywide implications
- intimate authority that never feels entirely transparent

They embody one of Lantern City’s core tensions:
light as care versus light as control.

## Primary Territory and District Footprint

### Strong district anchor
- Old Quarter

### Additional influence
- shrine lanes
- lantern maintenance nodes with ritual dependencies
- neighborhood sanctuaries
- ceremonial support spaces in Lantern Ward

### Practical district identity
In the Old Quarter, they are not the main record authority, but they often know what local systems are doing before archives or civic offices are willing to say it aloud.

## Resources and Assets

### Core assets
- shrine spaces
- ritual knowledge
- maintenance traditions and practical expertise
- local trust in affected neighborhoods
- ability to read lantern behavior beyond formal inspection language
- informal networks of attendants, aides, and neighborhood observers

### Less visible assets
- knowledge of which lanterns are politically sensitive
- memory of prior disturbances not fully captured in official records
- selective access to rituals, sigils, or practices outsiders do not understand
- the ability to distinguish ordinary failure from intentional alteration

### Asset type in play
Their strongest resource is interpretive authority.
They may not always certify official truth, but they often know what a lantern event means before anyone else does.

## Methods

The Shrine Circles prefer to act through:
- local guidance
- selective disclosure
- practical maintenance
- ritual interpretation
- neighborhood trust
- quiet warning
- stabilizing action rather than open declaration

They do not like being dragged into blunt institutional categories if those categories flatten the real situation.

## Public Language Style

Shrine Circle public language should sound:
- calm
- measured
- practical rather than theatrical
- suggestive without being uselessly vague
- respectful of danger without sensationalizing it

Typical phrasing patterns:
- “The light is not behaving as it should.”
- “That pattern is not ordinary neglect.”
- “Something touched the system before the complaint reached us.”
- “Stability and truth are not always restored in the same order.”
- “If you force the wrong answer too early, the district will live with it.”

## Internal Beliefs

A useful internal creed for the faction is:
- systems fail differently when they are damaged, neglected, or changed on purpose
- not every witness account should be trusted equally under unstable light
- ritual language is not decoration; it encodes practical knowledge
- city authorities often want technical calm without metaphysical honesty

This makes the Shrine Circles crucial for Lantern City’s signature mechanics.

## Relationships with Other Factions

### Memory Keepers
Relationship:
- tense interdependence

Why:
- archives need coherent lantern conditions to trust continuity
- shrines know continuity is partly environmental and ritual, not merely documentary
- each side thinks the other reduces reality too much

MVP expression:
- shrine actors suspect records are being used to manage outcomes
- record actors suspect shrine opacity makes everything harder to certify

### Council of Lights
Relationship:
- respectful rivalry with political danger underneath

Why:
- the Council wants regulation, compliance, and public clarity
- shrine actors know the system is older, stranger, and less fully regulatable than the Council prefers to admit
- both need each other in practice, and neither fully trusts the other’s framing

MVP expression:
- some shrine voices may accept quiet coordination
- others resent official control over systems the Council only partly understands

## Red Lines

The Shrine Circles become much more protective when:
- sacred or sensitive maintenance knowledge is exposed crudely
- a ritual anomaly is publicly framed as simple negligence
- officials try to force a false technical explanation onto a deeper event
- shrine workers are scapegoated for politically caused manipulation
- the player threatens stability without understanding the consequences

If pushed across these lines, they may:
- refuse deeper disclosure
- redirect the player away from critical knowledge
- offer only partial truth
- support quiet containment over public exposure

## What Success Looks Like for Them

A successful outcome for the Shrine Circles is:
- lantern stability restored or contained
- local people protected from avoidable panic or blame
- ritual knowledge not crudely weaponized against them
- the player or city learns enough to act correctly, but not necessarily enough to expose every hidden mechanism

This means they may support both clean exposure and quiet resolution depending on context.

## What Failure Looks Like for Them

Failure for the Shrine Circles means:
- ritual negligence becomes the public scapegoat regardless of truth
- local lantern systems degrade through misuse or denial
- political actors seize the narrative and flatten the event into a false explanation
- shrine authority is weakened while instability remains unresolved

## Role in The Missing Clerk Case

In the MVP case, the Shrine Circles should be the faction most associated with:
- the first true hint that the outage was deliberate
- practical lantern interpretation
- local trust and local fear
- the difference between failure and alteration
- the moral question of whether every truth should become public immediately

### Important rule
They should not be treated as pure “keepers of mystical answers.”
Their value is that they know real operational things the official system cannot easily say.

## Example MVP Positions Within the Faction

### Ila Venn type
- local practical witness
- knows the light is wrong before she knows why
- cautious because she understands blame will move toward the shrine first

### Sister Calis type
- higher-status interpretive authority
- understands broader political consequences
- willing to negotiate between truth and stability

### unseen shrine labor layer
- attendants, relighters, maintainers, aides
- useful future expansion channel for rumors, routines, and overlooked patterns

## Player-Facing Behaviors

The player should experience the Shrine Circles as a faction that:
- notices abnormal lantern patterns early
- gives meaningful but incomplete guidance
- rewards respectful, precise inquiry
- becomes more useful when the player proves observational competence
- may prefer careful containment over spectacle

## Reaction Logic

### If the player is observant and respectful
The faction becomes:
- quietly helpful
- more willing to interpret strange evidence
- more likely to grant access to workrooms, notes, or deeper context

### If the player is crude or accusatory
The faction becomes:
- guarded
- less explanatory
- more likely to redirect rather than reveal

### If the player understands both risk and truth
The faction may support:
- a careful exposure route
- a stabilization-first route
- a bargain that preserves life while deferring wider scandal

## What They Can Offer the Player

If engaged well, the Shrine Circles can provide:
- lantern interpretation
- local trust
- access to shrine spaces
- practical understanding of alteration signs
- insight into what evidence means under unstable light
- guidance on how to stabilize or safely approach a disturbed site

## What They Can Deny the Player

If engaged poorly, they can deny:
- deeper ritual explanation
- access to useful local spaces
- confidence in the player’s interpretations
- timely warning about what instability is doing

## Reputation Effects

Reputation with the Shrine Circles should rise when the player:
- distinguishes damage from manipulation
- protects local workers from careless blame
- listens before escalating publicly
- treats ritual practice as practical knowledge rather than superstition

Reputation should fall when the player:
- dismisses shrine knowledge as decorative
- exposes sensitive details recklessly
- turns local instability into public theater without understanding consequences

## Long-Term Faction Hook

For future cases, the Shrine Circles can anchor stories about:
- hidden lantern topologies
- ritual maintenance disputes
- neighborhoods held together by practices the city barely acknowledges
- old pacts between shrines and civic systems
- the cost of making intimate knowledge public

## Design Rules

1. The Shrine Circles should feel grounded, not floaty.
2. Their knowledge should be practical even when expressed in ritual language.
3. They should not solve mysteries for the player; they should sharpen interpretation.
4. Their opacity should feel protective and political at the same time.
5. They should embody the tension between care, secrecy, and authority.
6. They should make the player think about whether stability and disclosure are always aligned.

## Summary

The Shrine Circles are Lantern City’s faction of local ritual intelligence.
They care for the city’s intimate light systems and often understand disturbances before official institutions do.

In the MVP, they should be the faction that teaches the player how lantern events are read, not just observed.
