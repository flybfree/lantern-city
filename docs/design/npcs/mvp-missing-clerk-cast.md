# Lantern City — MVP NPC Cast Sheets

## Purpose

This document provides concrete NPC sheets for the MVP reference case, The Missing Clerk.
It turns the abstract NPC interaction system into a small authored cast that can support:
- the opening investigation loop
- clue distribution
- trust and suspicion changes
- redirection between scenes
- partial truth, hidden truth, and political pressure

These sheets should function as the canonical starting cast for the MVP.
They are not just lore notes.
They are gameplay-facing reference profiles.

## Cast Scope

This cast is designed for one medium MVP case with:
- one missing-person investigation
- one lantern alteration event
- one records discrepancy network
- one hidden-route discovery
- one final truth decision

The cast should be small enough to manage, but rich enough to prove the NPC system works.

## Shared Design Rules

For this case, every tracked NPC should answer these questions:
- What do they want right now?
- What are they hiding?
- What can they tell the player early?
- What must be earned or forced out of them?
- What clue or route do they connect to?
- How do they react to trust, pressure, evidence, or delay?

No NPC should exist only to dump exposition.
Each one should:
- hold a different slice of the truth
- redirect the player somewhere meaningful
- react to prior actions
- create some form of social or investigative texture

## Relationship Summary

At a high level:
- Tovin feared Sered Marr’s office and trusted the records less than the lantern evidence.
- Ila Venn suspects Sered is suppressing access, but does not know the entire chain.
- Brin Hesse knows a maintenance route was used off schedule and fears both official and local punishment.
- Sister Calis knows more about the ritual signature than she admits and prefers containment.
- Watcher Pell wants a clean administrative close and will lean toward whichever story seems easiest to certify.

## 1. Tovin Vale

### Core identity
- Role category: absent lead / hidden victim
- Public identity: junior archive clerk
- District anchor: Old Quarter
- Initial availability: unavailable until breakthrough
- Case function: missing person, truth witness, human proof of the case

### Surface read
Tovin is remembered, if at all, as quiet, competent, and too careful to vanish casually.
People who truly knew him think he was anxious in recent days.
People closer to the altered lantern field may hesitate, misremember him, or speak as though he belonged only half to the office.

### What Tovin wants
- to survive
- to make sure the copied proof is not lost
- to know whether the player is actually safer than the institutions around him

### What Tovin is hiding
- he copied more than one discrepancy, not just his own
- he delayed going public because he was not sure who was involved
- he may have seen an identifying seal, phrase, or signature that points beyond one careless clerk

### What Tovin knows early, if found
- the discrepancy was deliberate
- the lantern issue began before his disappearance
- someone used records control and light instability together

### What must be earned from Tovin
- the full sequence of what he discovered
- the strongest proof he preserved
- the degree to which the event may connect to a broader records-editing pattern

### Emotional profile
- exhausted
- frightened
- ashamed that he hid instead of exposing the truth sooner
- still stubborn enough to want the record corrected

### How he reacts to player behavior
If the player is careful, specific, and protective:
- Tovin becomes cooperative quickly
- he shares the copy sheet and what he overheard

If the player is grandiose, reckless, or publicly confrontational:
- Tovin fears being used as evidence rather than rescued
- he withholds part of what he knows

### Clue links
- hidden copy sheet
- direct testimony on altered records
- evidence of broader pattern

### Scene utility
Tovin should not simply end the case on contact.
Finding him shifts the case from investigation into resolution and truth management.

## 2. Ila Venn

### Core identity
- Role category: informant leaning lead
- Public identity: shrine keeper on Shrine Lane
- District anchor: Old Quarter
- Initial availability: available early
- Case function: first real lantern-informed witness

### Surface read
Ila is composed, observant, and too controlled to be casual.
She notices what the player is asking before answering it.
She seems tired in the specific way of someone holding a system together by routine.

### What Ila wants
- to keep the local lantern from destabilizing further
- to prevent blame from falling entirely on the shrine
- to see the truth handled by someone more capable than the archive office

### What Ila is hiding
- she recognized signs of deliberate alteration early
- she chose not to report everything immediately because she feared who would control the response
- she may know the ritual signature resembles sanctioned work more than vandalism

### What Ila can reveal early
- the outage predates the official disappearance report
- the lantern damage does not look ordinary
- Tovin came to her worried and asking about irregular maintenance
- someone official is telling the story backward

### What must be earned from Ila
- how much she really knows about the nature of the alteration
- the strongest practical interpretation of the lantern evidence
- access to a shrine workroom, maintenance notation, or deeper ritual hint

### Emotional profile
- guarded
- pragmatic
- guilty about waiting too long
- more willing to trust competence than rank

### Trust triggers
Increase trust when the player:
- notices physical evidence instead of demanding explanation first
- asks precise questions about timing or maintenance
- protects her from premature blame
- returns with corroboration instead of accusation

Decrease trust when the player:
- treats shrine maintenance as superstition
- publicly blames her too early
- drags authority in without evidence

### Pressure response
Under moderate pressure, Ila becomes terse but still useful.
Under high pressure, she may stop volunteering ritual detail and redirect the player toward physical evidence only.

### Clue links
- outage predates disappearance
- lantern bracket heat scoring
- false repair pattern

### Scene utility
Ila should teach the player how Lantern City conversations work at their best:
- short but meaningful
- full of implication
- immediately useful
- still incomplete

## 3. Sered Marr

### Core identity
- Role category: gatekeeper
- Public identity: acting archive registrar
- District anchor: Archive Steps / ledger room
- Initial availability: available from opening scene
- Case function: institutional obstruction, access gate, political resolution target

### Surface read
Sered is efficient, formal, and faintly irritated before the player has earned any irritation.
He speaks like someone who wants the scope of the problem reduced by grammar alone.
He is not obviously villainous.
He is obviously invested in containment.

### What Sered wants
- to preserve archive credibility
- to keep the incident from becoming a scandal
- to avoid superiors discovering how compromised local records became

### What Sered is hiding
- access restrictions are being used to manage perception, not just order
- one maintenance or registry line has been intentionally suppressed
- he already knows the official timeline does not hold together

### What Sered can reveal early
- Tovin’s presence in the office is under review
- records are inconsistent
- the situation is not appropriate for public speculation
- shrine maintenance may be relevant

### What must be earned or forced from Sered
- access to deeper records
- admission that the office suppressed a discrepancy
- confirmation that a maintenance line is missing
- a practical opening to the hidden route or restricted room network

### Emotional profile
- brittle
- proud
- anxious beneath composure
- protective of institutional legitimacy above personal warmth

### Trust triggers
Sered does not trust easily.
He responds better to:
- procedural competence
- evidence presented cleanly
- demonstrated understanding of how embarrassing the issue is

### Pressure triggers
Sered becomes more useful when the player has:
- contradictory document proof
- maintenance evidence
- witness corroboration from outside the affected lantern field
- leverage that threatens official embarrassment

### Failure mode
If mishandled too early, Sered may:
- clamp down on access
- call in Watcher Pell
- harden the office into a false official version

### Clue links
- contradictory roster entry
- missing maintenance line
- ledger hand mismatch

### Scene utility
Sered is a good example of a Lantern City NPC whose value is not kindness, but pressure.
He blocks, narrows, deflects, and eventually yields in highly informative ways.

## 4. Brin Hesse

### Core identity
- Role category: world NPC promoted to meaningful informant
- Public identity: lamplighter’s assistant / maintenance runner
- District anchor: route between Shrine Lane and service access points
- Initial availability: discoverable after first lantern or records lead
- Case function: practical bridge between abstract suspicion and physical route evidence

### Surface read
Brin looks like someone who learned to move quickly through systems he does not control.
He is evasive in the ordinary way of a worker used to blame traveling downhill.
He does not initially seem important.
That is deliberate.

### What Brin wants
- to avoid punishment
- to avoid becoming the one name everyone agrees to blame
- to stay useful to whoever still controls maintenance work

### What Brin is hiding
- he passed along a key, note, or entry token off schedule
- he saw enough to know the repair was not routine
- he suspects the order came from someone protected

### What Brin can reveal early
- a route was opened when it should not have been
- someone used maintenance authority irregularly
- official explanations are too neat

### What must be earned from Brin
- who requested the irregular access
- what exact object or key changed hands
- where the service route actually leads

### Emotional profile
- nervous
- self-protective
- observant
- more honest when treated like a worker caught between powers rather than a liar

### Trust triggers
Increase trust when the player:
- avoids threatening him immediately
- shows evidence that he is not the main actor
- offers discretion
- demonstrates awareness of how maintenance labor is used as cover

Decrease trust when the player:
- accuses him too early
- implies he is disposable
- brings authority pressure before rapport

### Clue links
- missing maintenance line
- off-schedule route access
- hidden passage discovery

### Scene utility
Brin turns the case from contradiction into movement.
He is the character most likely to connect paperwork and lantern evidence to an actual route through space.

## 5. Sister Calis

### Core identity
- Role category: secondary lead / faction voice
- Public identity: senior shrine ritualist or lantern liaison
- District anchor: shrine authority node or Lantern Ward contact point
- Initial availability: mid-case, after player proves seriousness
- Case function: expands the case from local anomaly to controlled system

### Surface read
Calis is calm in a way that feels curated.
She has the language of public stability and the instincts of someone who knows what must never be said plainly.
She is not easily rattled, but she is always measuring what version of the truth is survivable.

### What Calis wants
- to keep ritual legitimacy intact
- to avoid exposing shrine methods to hostile oversight
- to contain the event within negotiable boundaries

### What Calis is hiding
- the alteration pattern resembles sanctioned knowledge, even if misused
- parts of the system are vulnerable in ways the public must never hear directly
- the player may be correct, but full exposure could trigger broader instability

### What Calis can reveal early
- the lantern signature is not random damage
- this kind of manipulation takes knowledge, not chance
- some truths can be corrected quietly faster than publicly

### What must be earned from Calis
- confirmation that the alteration was deliberate and technically sophisticated
- understanding of what the altered field was capable of doing
- admission that the case may connect to a broader institutional pattern

### Emotional profile
- disciplined
- politically intelligent
- sincere about stability, but not neutral about truth

### Trust triggers
Calis respects:
- composure
- careful interpretation
- demonstrated ability to keep a confidence when necessary

### Pressure triggers
Calis responds when the player shows:
- strong evidence
- ability to damage multiple institutions if ignored
- understanding that order and truth are now in tension

### Clue links
- ritual signature interpretation
- confirmation of deliberate alteration
- political bargain route

### Scene utility
Calis should make the player feel the larger game without requiring a larger MVP scope.
She is where the case becomes visibly about memory governance, not only a missing clerk.

## 6. Watcher Pell

### Core identity
- Role category: authority pressure NPC
- Public identity: district civic watch officer
- District anchor: archive-adjacent or administrative edge site
- Initial availability: available if authority is invoked or if Sered escalates
- Case function: shows how ambiguous truth becomes certified falsehood

### Surface read
Pell is tired, procedural, and impatient with anything that threatens paperwork volume.
He is not cruel.
He is the kind of official who can do harm by preferring a clean report over a true one.

### What Pell wants
- to close the complaint with minimal escalation
- to avoid cross-office contradiction
- to keep his own certification intact

### What Pell is hiding
- he already suspects the record situation is irregular
- he would prefer a plausible false close to a complicated true one
- he can be moved by evidence, but only if it becomes harder to ignore than certify away

### What Pell can reveal early
- the office would prefer this logged as an unclear absence
- official channels are narrowing the case already
- if the player cannot produce clean proof, authority will settle for administrative ambiguity

### What must be earned from Pell
- willingness to reopen or refuse a false closure
- formal pressure against Sered or another responsible node
- procedural legitimacy for a clean-exposure route

### Emotional profile
- worn down
- pragmatic
- conflict-averse
- morally weak rather than actively malicious

### Clue links
- official closure pressure
- false administrative resolution risk
- route to procedural legitimacy if convinced

### Scene utility
Pell is the warning sign for the player:
if they do nothing, the city will decide for them.

## NPC Escalation Logic

These NPCs should not remain static.
Their behavior should change based on player progress.

### Ila Venn escalation
- Early: cautious but useful
- Mid: more direct if respected
- Late: willing to risk deeper disclosure if the player proves competent

### Sered Marr escalation
- Early: denial and narrowing
- Mid: defensive proceduralism
- Late: partial admission, bargain, or panic depending on leverage

### Brin Hesse escalation
- Early: evasive minor worker
- Mid: frightened witness
- Late: route unlock or explicit corroborator

### Sister Calis escalation
- Early: distant authority
- Mid: controlled acknowledgement
- Late: bargain-maker or reluctant confirmer

### Watcher Pell escalation
- Early: routine close-out pressure
- Mid: administrative threat
- Late: either procedural ally or certifier of the false version

## Relationship and Memory Notes

The game should track at least these relational shifts:
- Ila trusts the player more if they interpret evidence well
- Sered becomes more guarded if publicly embarrassed
- Brin becomes more honest if protected from scapegoating
- Calis becomes more candid if the player shows discretion and competence
- Pell becomes more cooperative only when evidence is cleaner than denial
- Tovin becomes more open if he believes the player will protect both him and the proof

## Why This Cast Works for the MVP

This set works because each NPC serves a distinct gameplay function:
- Ila introduces lantern inference
- Sered controls institutional access
- Brin links suspicion to space and movement
- Calis introduces managed truth and political stakes
- Pell represents administrative closure pressure
- Tovin converts theory into human truth and final decision

Together they create:
- one strong opening triangle
- multiple mid-case routes
- social and evidentiary progression
- a meaningful final resolution choice

## Summary

This NPC cast is the authored social backbone of the MVP reference case.
It provides the minimum set of memorable, playable, and systemically useful characters needed to make The Missing Clerk feel like a real Lantern City investigation.

These sheets should be treated as a reference standard for future case casts:
small, distinct, clue-linked, pressure-sensitive, and built to support both mystery and consequence.
